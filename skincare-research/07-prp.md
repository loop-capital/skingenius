# PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma): Comprehensive Guide

## What it is
An autologous treatment that concentrates platelets from the patient's own blood and reintroduces them into the skin via injection or microneedling, delivering a high concentration of growth factors and cytokines that stimulate tissue regeneration, collagen production, and wound healing.

## Best for
- Skin rejuvenation and "glow" improvement
- Fine lines and wrinkles
- Acne scarring (especially atrophic scars)
- Under-eye hollows and dark circles
- Hair loss (androgenetic alopecia, telogen effluvium)
- Stretch marks
- Uneven skin texture and tone
- Post-procedure healing enhancement (combined with microneedling, lasers)
- Mild skin laxity
- Periorbital rejuvenation
- Scar improvement (surgical, traumatic)
- Alopecia areata (scalp PRP)

## How it works
PRP leverages the body's own healing mechanisms by concentrating platelets 3-5x above baseline:

1. **Blood draw:** 10-60ml of the patient's blood is collected
2. **Centrifugation:** Blood is spun to separate platelet-rich plasma from red blood cells and platelet-poor plasma
3. **Activation:** Platelets are activated (via calcium chloride, thrombin, or mechanical means) to release growth factors
4. **Delivery:** PRP is injected or applied topically with microneedling into the treatment area
5. **Growth factor release:** Platelets release PDGF, TGF-β, VEGF, EGF, FGF, and IGF-1
6. **Biological cascade:**
   - **PDGF** (Platelet-Derived Growth Factor): Stimulates fibroblast proliferation and collagen synthesis
   - **TGF-β** (Transforming Growth Factor Beta): Promotes extracellular matrix production
   - **VEGF** (Vascular Endothelial Growth Factor): Angiogenesis, improves blood supply
   - **EGF** (Epidermal Growth Factor): Epidermal regeneration and wound healing
   - **FGF** (Fibroblast Growth Factor): Cell proliferation and tissue repair
   - **IGF-1** (Insulin-Like Growth Factor): Cell growth and differentiation
7. **Neovascularization and collagen remodeling** occur over weeks to months

## Longevity/Duration
- **Skin rejuvenation:** Results visible 2-4 weeks post-treatment, peak at 3-6 months
- **Maintenance:** 3-4 initial sessions spaced 4-6 weeks apart, then every 6-12 months
- **Hair growth:** Visible improvement 2-3 months, peak at 6-12 months
- **Hair maintenance:** Every 3-6 months after initial series
- **Under-eye improvement:** Immediate volume effect from plasma, long-term collagen stimulation over 3-6 months
- **Note:** Results are cumulative; series of treatments recommended for optimal benefit
- **Note:** Autologous nature means results vary with individual platelet quality, age, and health status

## Pros
1. **Autologous** — uses patient's own blood, eliminating risk of allergic reaction or disease transmission
2. **Natural growth factors** — harnesses body's own healing and regenerative mechanisms
3. **Minimal downtime** — especially compared to lasers or deep peels
4. **Versatile** — face, scalp, body, under-eyes, neck, décolleté
5. **Combination-friendly** — enhances results of microneedling, lasers, and fillers
6. **Low risk profile** — autologous product reduces complication concerns
7. **No synthetic materials** — no foreign substances injected
8. **Improves skin quality** — texture, tone, elasticity, and luminosity
9. **Hair growth stimulation** — evidence-based for androgenetic alopecia
10. **Under-eye improvement** — one of few treatments effective for dark circles/hollows
11. **Progressive improvement** — collagen stimulation continues over months
12. **Can be repeated safely** — no cumulative toxicity or material buildup
13. **FDA-cleared preparation systems** — multiple devices cleared for platelet concentration
14. **Anti-inflammatory** — PRP modulates inflammation (especially LP-PRP)
15. **No pre/post-treatment product requirements** — unlike some laser or peel protocols

## Cons
1. **Variable results** — efficacy depends on individual platelet quality, age, health status
2. **Requires blood draw** — needle-phobic patients may object
3. **Multiple sessions needed** — optimal results require 3-4 treatments
4. **Cost** — can be expensive over a series of treatments
5. **Limited evidence** — fewer large-scale RCTs than fillers or lasers; some studies show no significant difference vs. microneedling alone
6. **No immediate results** — improvement develops over weeks to months
7. **Platelet quality varies** — affected by age, medications, health status, smoking
8. **Preparation variability** — different centrifuge systems yield different concentrations
9. **Bruising** — common at injection sites (resolves 5-7 days)
10. **Swelling** — especially under-eyes, can last 1-3 days
11. **Discomfort during injection** — requires topical or local anesthesia
12. **Not a volume replacement** — cannot replace significant volume loss like fillers
13. **Not regulated uniformly** — preparation methods vary widely between providers
14. **Limited standalone efficacy** — best results in combination with other modalities
15. **Contraindicated** in certain blood disorders, active infections, anticoagulant use

## Evidence Tier
**Moderate** — Growing evidence base with in vitro mechanistic data (growth factors well-characterized) and multiple clinical studies showing benefit. However, evidence quality is limited by: small sample sizes, heterogeneous preparation methods, lack of standardized protocols, and some RCTs showing no significant difference vs. microneedling alone. Strongest evidence for hair loss (androgenetic alopecia) and acne scarring (in combination with microneedling). Weakest evidence as standalone anti-aging treatment.

## Downtime
- **PRP Injection (face):**
  - Immediate: Mild swelling, redness, possible bruising
  - Swelling duration: 12-48 hours (longer under eyes, 1-3 days)
  - Bruising: 5-7 days (variable)
  - Makeup: 6-12 hours post-treatment
  - Activity: Normal next day; avoid strenuous exercise 24-48 hours
  - Full social recovery: 1-3 days
- **PRP + Microneedling:**
  - Immediate: Redness, mild swelling, pinpoint bleeding
  - Redness: 24-48 hours (may last 3-5 days with deeper treatment)
  - Swelling: 12-24 hours (longer around eyes)
  - Bruising: 5-7 days
  - Makeup: 24 hours (mineral)
  - Activity: Normal 24-48 hours
  - Full social recovery: 1-3 days
- **Scalp PRP:**
  - Immediate: Mild tenderness, possible swelling at injection sites
  - Activity: Normal same day
  - Hair washing: 12-24 hours post-treatment
  - Full recovery: 0-1 days
- **Results Timeline:**
  - Initial improvement: 2-4 weeks
  - Progressive improvement: 1-3 months
  - Optimal results: 3-6 months (after series completion)

## Risks + Safety Findings
**Common:** Injection site pain (70-80%), bruising (40-60%), swelling (50-70%), redness (80-90%)
**Uncommon:** Prolonged swelling >72 hours (5-10%), hematoma (2-5%), infection at injection site (<1%), allergic reaction to topical anesthetic (3-5%)
**Rare but Important:**
- **Infection:** Bacterial or viral if sterile technique compromised (<0.1% with proper technique)
- **Nerve injury:** Theoretical risk with deep injection near nerves
- **Vascular compromise:** Extremely rare with proper technique; blind injection in periorbital area carries risk
- **Unsatisfactory results:** Variable response; some patients see minimal improvement
- **Skin discoloration:** Rare post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation
- **Granuloma formation:** Extremely rare with autologous PRP

**Key Safety Considerations:**
- **Autologous only** — patient's own blood eliminates risk of disease transmission or allergic reaction to PRP itself
- **Sterile technique essential** — blood products require careful handling
- **FDA regulation** — PRP preparation systems are FDA-cleared, but PRP for cosmetic use is off-label
- **Blood draw required** — patients on anticoagulants, with blood disorders, or anemia may not be candidates
- **Avoid if:** Active infection, blood dyscrasias, anticoagulant therapy, immunosuppression, pregnancy
- **Pre-treatment:** Avoid NSAIDs, aspirin, alcohol 5-7 days before to optimize platelet function
- **Hydration matters** — adequate hydration improves blood draw quality
- **Platelet quality** — affected by smoking, poor nutrition, chronic disease, certain medications
- **Variable preparation** — different centrifuge systems and protocols yield different platelet concentrations
- **Infection risk in 2019** — CDC investigated HIV and hepatitis cases linked to improper PRP practices; sterile technique is non-negotiable

## When to Recommend vs Skincare Alone
**RECOMMEND PRP WHEN:**
- Acne scarring not adequately responding to microneedling alone
- Under-eye hollows or dark circles where fillers are contraindicated or undesired
- Hair thinning or early androgenetic alopecia (evidence-based for scalp PRP)
- Patient seeking natural/autologous treatment (no foreign substances)
- Post-procedure enhancement (combined with microneedling for synergistic effect)
- Mild skin laxity and texture concerns
- Patient prefers progressive, natural-looking improvement
- Combination approach planned (PRP + microneedling, PRP + laser, PRP + fillers)
- Patient unable or unwilling to use synthetic fillers
- Chronic wound healing concerns
- Adjuvant treatment for melasma (anti-inflammatory benefit)
- Patient has realistic expectations about gradual improvement

**SKINCARE ALONE MAY BE SUFFICIENT WHEN:**
- Early photoaging responsive to retinoids, antioxidants, and sunscreen
- Mild texture concerns improving with medical-grade skincare
- Patient cannot commit to multiple sessions needed for optimal results
- Budget constraints — quality topical skincare may provide more predictable results per dollar
- Significant volume loss requiring fillers or surgical intervention
- Active skin infection, blood disorder, or immunosuppression
- Anticoagulant therapy that cannot be temporarily discontinued
- Anemia or thrombocytopenia that limits platelet yield
- Needle phobia or aversion to blood draw
- Unrealistic expectations about degree of improvement from PRP alone
- Patient seeking immediate, dramatic results
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding (limited safety data)
- Recent isotretinoin use (<6 months)

**CLINICAL PEARLS:**
- **Preparation matters:** Ensure adequate hydration 24-48 hours before treatment
- **Medication review:** Discontinue NSAIDs, aspirin, alcohol 5-7 days prior for optimal platelet function
- **Fasting not required** — but avoid high-fat meals before blood draw
- **Centrifugation protocol** — LP-PRP preferred for facial rejuvenation (less inflammatory); LR-PRP preferred for hair and wounds
- **Anesthesia:** Topical lidocaine 30-45 minutes before treatment; nerve blocks for scalp
- **Microneedling synergy:** PRP applied topically during microneedling enhances penetration 3-5x
- **Injection vs. topical:** Injection delivers deeper, more targeted results; topical with microneedling provides broader coverage
- **Series is key:** 3-4 sessions at 4-6 week intervals for optimal results
- **Maintenance required:** 1-2 sessions per year after initial series
- **Age factor:** Younger patients tend to have better platelet quality and response
- **Smoking cessation:** Smoking impairs platelet function; recommend cessation for optimal results
- **Combine wisely:** PRP + microneedling > either alone; PRP + laser accelerates healing and enhances results
- **Set expectations:** Results are gradual, cumulative, and variable; not equivalent to fillers for volume

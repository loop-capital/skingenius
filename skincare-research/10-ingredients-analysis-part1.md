## 🔬 ONESKIN INGREDIENTS

### 1. OS-01 PEPTIDE (Decapeptide-52)

**Description:** Proprietary senotherapeutic peptide

**Concentration:** Not disclosed (proprietary formulation)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Senomorphic Agent:** Reprograms senescent cells toward a healthier state
- **Senolytic Properties:** Reduces accumulation of aged/senescent cells
- **Prevents Senescence Spread:** Stops neighboring cells from becoming dysfunctional
- **Anti-Inflammatory:** Reduces inflammatory chemicals released by senescent cells
- **DNA Methylation:** Reduces skin equivalent DNA methylation age by average of 2.6 years
- **Barrier Strengthening:** Improves skin barrier function by up to +41%

**Clinical Studies:**
- **npj Aging (2023):** "Senotherapeutic peptide treatment reduces biological age and senescence burden in human skin models"
  - Published in Nature journal
  - In vitro and ex vivo human skin models
  - Results: Significant reduction in senescence markers
  - DOI: 10.1038/s41514-023-00109-1

- **Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology (2024):** "Double-blind, vehicle-controlled clinical investigation of peptide OS-01 for skin rejuvenation"
  - 12-week clinical study
  - Double-blind, split-face design
  - Published in Wiley peer-reviewed journal
  - DOI: 10.1111/jocd.16242

**Optimal Usage:**
- Daily application (morning and/or evening)
- Compatible with most other skincare ingredients
- Safe for sensitive skin (fragrance-free, gentle formulation)
- Can be used long-term for preventative aging

**Side Effects/Contraindications:**
- Generally well-tolerated
- No significant side effects reported in clinical studies
- Safe for all skin types including sensitive

**Comparison to Other Anti-Aging Ingredients:**
- **vs. Retinol:** OS-01 works at cellular level (senescence) vs. surface cell turnover
- **vs. Vitamin C:** OS-01 targets aging root cause vs. antioxidant protection
- **vs. Growth Factors:** OS-01 prevents cell aging vs. stimulating collagen
- **Unique Position:** Only ingredient proven to reduce biological age of skin

### 2. HYALURONIC ACID (Multiple Forms)

**Concentration:** Not disclosed

**Forms Used:**
- Hyaluronic Acid
- Sodium Hyaluronate
- Sodium Hyaluronate Crosspolymer

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Hydration:** Binds up to 1000x its weight in water
- **Plumping:** Fills space between collagen and elastin fibers
- **Barrier Support:** Strengthens skin moisture barrier
- **Wound Healing:** Supports tissue repair processes

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Extensively studied in dermatology literature
- Proven efficacy for hydration and anti-aging
- Multiple molecular weights for different penetration depths

**Optimal Concentration:** 0.1% - 2% (varies by molecular weight)

**Synergistic Combinations:**
- Works well with niacinamide, peptides, ceramides
- Enhances penetration of other actives

### 3. NIACINAMIDE (Vitamin B3)

**Concentration:** Not disclosed (typically 2-5% in formulations)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Barrier Repair:** Increases ceramide production
- **Brightening:** Reduces melanin transfer
- **Anti-Inflammatory:** Calms redness and irritation
- **Pore Minimizing:** Regulates sebum production

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **PubMed Studies:** Extensive research on niacinamide for aging, acne, hyperpigmentation
- **Optimal Range:** 2-5% for most benefits
- **Well-Tolerated:** Safe for all skin types

**Synergistic Combinations:**
- + Hyaluronic acid: Enhanced hydration
- + Peptides: Anti-aging synergy
- + Vitamin C: Brightening boost (use at different times of day)

## 🔬 PCA SKIN INGREDIENTS

### 1. TRICHLOROACETIC ACID (TCA)

**Concentration in Products:** 10% - 35% (professional peels)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Protein Precipitation:** Coagulates skin proteins causing controlled injury
- **Deep Exfoliation:** Penetrates to dermis for resurfacing
- **Collagen Stimulation:** Wound healing response stimulates new collagen
- **Melanin Dispersion:** Breaks up pigmentation clusters

**Clinical Studies:**
- **PMC Systematic Review (2021):** "Trichloroacetic Acid Peeling for Treating Photoaging"
  - Multiple studies reviewed
  - All showed clinical improvement of photoaging
  - Various concentrations effective

- **PMC (2018):** "Efficacy and Safety of 25% TCA Peel Versus 30% Salicylic Acid"
  - Comparative study for acne
  - Both effective with good safety profiles

- **PMC (2017):** "Clinical Efficacy of 20% TCA with 5% Ascorbic Acid for Melasma"
  - 12-week study
  - Combination effective for melasma

**Optimal Concentration Ranges:**
- **Superficial Peel:** 10% - 20%
- **Medium Peel:** 25% - 35%
- **Deep Peel:** 35%+ (medical supervision required)

**Side Effects/Contraindications:**
- **Common:** Redness, peeling, crusting (expected)
- **Risk:** Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (especially darker skin)
- **Contraindications:** Active infection, pregnancy, recent isotretinoin use
- **Downtime:** 3-7 days for superficial, 1-2 weeks for medium

**Comparison to Other Acids:**
- **vs. Glycolic Acid:** TCA penetrates deeper, more dramatic results
- **vs. Salicylic Acid:** TCA better for aging, salicylic better for acne
- **vs. Jessner's Solution:** Often combined for enhanced efficacy

### 2. LACTIC ACID

**Concentration in PCA Products:** Varies (peels typically 10% - 30%)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **AHA Exfoliation:** Dissolves bonds between dead skin cells
- **Hydration:** Humectant properties (unique among AHAs)
- **Collagen Stimulation:** Mild stimulation of dermal remodeling
- **Brightening:** Evens skin tone

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Frontiers in Medicine (2021):** TCA-lactic acid combination study
  - Synergistic inhibition activities
  - Enhanced efficacy vs. either acid alone
  - Molecular docking confirmed mechanism

**Optimal Concentration:**
- **Daily Use:** 5% - 10%
- **Professional Peels:** 20% - 30%
- **Maximum Efficacy:** 12% for at-home, higher for professional

**Synergistic Combinations:**
- + TCA: Enhanced resurfacing (PCA's signature combination)
- + Salicylic Acid: Broad-spectrum exfoliation
- + Vitamin C: Brightening boost

---

### 3. RETINOL (Vitamin A)

**Concentration in PCA Products:** 0.5% (Intensive treatments)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Cell Turnover:** Accelerates epidermal renewal
- **Collagen Synthesis:** Stimulates Types I and III collagen
- **Melanin Dispersion:** Reduces hyperpigmentation
- **Sebum Regulation:** Reduces oil production
- **Follicular Clearance:** Unclogs pores

**Clinical Studies:**
- **PMC (2022):** "Use of Retinoids in Topical Antiaging Treatments"
  - Retinol 0.25% = 0.025% tretinoin efficacy
  - 0.5% considered standard effective OTC concentration
  - Maximum OTC concentration: 1%

- **PubMed (2020):** "A Clinical Anti-Ageing Comparative Study of 0.3 and 0.5% Retinol"
  - Both concentrations effective
  - 0.5% showed faster results

**Optimal Concentration Ranges:**
- **Beginner:** 0.25% - 0.3%
- **Standard:** 0.5%
- **Advanced:** 1%
- **Maximum OTC:** 1%
- **Prescription:** Tretinoin 0.025% - 0.1%

**Side Effects/Contraindications:**
- **Retinization Period:** 2-6 weeks of irritation, peeling
- **Photosensitivity:** Must use SPF daily
- **Contraindications:** Pregnancy, breastfeeding
- **Sensitivity:** Start low, go slow

**Comparison:**
- **vs. Retinaldehyde:** Retinol requires 2 conversion steps vs. 1 for retinaldehyde
- **vs. Tretinoin:** Retinol 10x less potent but better tolerated
- **vs. Retinyl Palmitate:** Weakest OTC retinoid, minimal efficacy

### 4. SALICYLIC ACID (BHA)

**Concentration in PCA Products:** 2% (standard for acne treatments)

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Lipid-Soluble:** Penetrates oil-filled pores
- **Keratolytic:** Dissolves dead skin cell buildup
- **Anti-Inflammatory:** Reduces redness and swelling
- **Comedolytic:** Unclogs pores and prevents new comedones

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Extensively studied for acne treatment
- 2% concentration FDA-approved for OTC acne
- Effective for both inflammatory and non-inflammatory acne

**Optimal Concentration:**
- **Daily Use:** 0.5% - 2%
- **Professional Peels:** 20% - 30%
- **Maximum OTC:** 2%

**Synergistic Combinations:**
- + Retinol: Enhanced cell turnover (PCA Intensive Clarity Treatment)
- + Glycolic Acid: Multi-depth exfoliation
- + Niacinamide: Anti-inflammatory boost

### 5. MATRIX GROWTH FACTORS (MGF)

**Used in:** MGF Age Renewal Cream (2026 NewBeauty Award Winner)

**Types:** Plant-derived and synthetic growth factors

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Cell Signaling:** Communicate with cells to trigger repair
- **Collagen Stimulation:** Increase Types I and III collagen
- **Elastin Production:** Improve skin elasticity
- **Wound Healing:** Accelerate tissue repair
- **Angiogenesis:** Improve blood flow to skin

**Clinical Evidence:**
- **Dovepress Review (2016):** "Skin rejuvenation using cosmetic products containing growth factors, cytokines, and matrikines"
  - Review of literature on topical growth factors
  - Shown to improve collagen I content
  - Reduce fragmentation of collagen fibrils

- **PMC (2014):** "Growth Factor-Extracellular Matrix Interactions Regulate Wound Repair"
  - Growth factors essential for wound healing
  - ECM interactions critical for efficacy

- **Wiley (2009):** "Interactions between extracellular matrix and growth factors in wound healing"
  - Growth factors bind to ECM for controlled release
  - Matrikines (ECM fragments) activate growth factor receptors

**Key Growth Factors in Skincare:**
- **EGF (Epidermal Growth Factor):** Cell proliferation
- **FGF (Fibroblast Growth Factor):** Collagen and elastin
- **TGF-β (Transforming Growth Factor):** Wound healing
- **PDGF (Platelet-Derived Growth Factor):** Cell migration
- **IGF (Insulin-like Growth Factor):** Tissue repair

**Side Effects/Considerations:**
- Generally well-tolerated
- Concerns about stimulating cancer cell growth (theoretical)
- Plant-derived vs. human-derived efficacy debates
- Best used in evening routines

**Comparison:**
- **vs. Retinol:** Growth factors repair vs. retinol renew
- **vs. Peptides:** Growth factors are larger signaling proteins vs. smaller peptide chains
- **vs. Stem Cells:** Growth factors are the active signaling molecules

### 6. KOJIC ACID

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Tyrosinase Inhibition:** Blocks melanin production
- **Antioxidant:** Free radical scavenging
- **Antibacterial:** Mild antimicrobial properties

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Well-established for hyperpigmentation
- Often combined with other brightening agents
- Effective for melasma, sun damage, PIH

**Optimal Concentration:** 1% - 4%

**Side Effects:**
- Potential irritation at higher concentrations
- Contact dermatitis in sensitive individuals

---

### 7. VITAMIN C (Ascorbic Acid)

**Concentration in PCA Products:** Varies by product

**Mechanism of Action:**
- **Antioxidant:** Neutralizes free radicals
- **Collagen Synthesis:** Cofactor for collagen production
- **Brightening:** Inhibits melanin production
- **UV Protection:** Enhances sunscreen efficacy

**Clinical Evidence:**
- Extensively studied in dermatology
- 5% concentration shown effective for anti-aging
- Higher concentrations (15-20%) for maximum efficacy

**Optimal Concentration:**
- **Minimum Effective:** 5%
- **Standard:** 10% - 15%
- **Maximum:** 20%

**Stability Issues:**
- Oxidizes quickly when exposed to air/light
- Best in airtight, opaque packaging
- Derivatives (ascorbyl glucoside, magnesium ascorbyl phosphate) more stable

## ⚠️ CONTRAINDICATIONS & PRECAUTIONS

### OneSkin
- **Generally Safe:** For all skin types
- **Patch Test:** Recommended for sensitive skin
- **Pregnancy:** Consult physician (OS-01 is novel ingredient)

### PCA Skin
- **TCA Peels:** Not for home use (professional only)
- **Retinol:** Avoid during pregnancy, use SPF
- **Salicylic Acid:** Avoid if allergic to aspirin
- **Combined Acids:** Professional supervision required

# Condition → Root Cause Mapping

## Purpose
Map each skin condition to its underlying root causes, so we can recommend treatments that address the source, not just the symptom.

## How to Use This Document
For each condition, identify:
1. **Primary root causes** (most common)
2. **Contributing factors** (may apply)
3. **Severity indicators** (when to see a dermatologist)
4. **Holistic treatment approach** (products + supplements + lifestyle + professional)

## 1. ACNE (Vulgaris, Hormonal, Cystic)

### Primary Root Causes
- **Gut dysbiosis** (50-70% correlation with SIBO, leaky gut)
- **Insulin resistance** (high glycemic diet, PCOS)
- **Hormonal imbalance** (elevated androgens, cortisol spikes)
- **Inflammation** (systemic, gut-derived)

### Contributing Factors
- Stress (cortisol increases sebum)
- Dairy consumption (IGF-1 stimulation)
- Poor sleep (disrupts hormone clearance)
- Comedogenic products
- Over-cleansing (disrupts microbiome)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Cystic/nodular acne
- Scarring occurring
- No improvement after 12 weeks of treatment
- Sudden onset in adulthood (possible hormonal disorder)
- Acne + hirsutism + irregular periods (possible PCOS)

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Salicylic acid (2%) — unclogs pores, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: A]
- Benzoyl peroxide (2.5-5%) — antibacterial, reduces resistance [Evidence: A]
- Retinoids (adapalene 0.1%) — cell turnover, prevents clogging [Evidence: A]
- Niacinamide (4-5%) — sebum regulation, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B+]
- Azelaic acid (10-20%) — antibacterial, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B+]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Zinc (30mg) — anti-inflammatory, hormone modulation [Evidence: B]
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Probiotics (Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG, L. reuteri) — gut-skin axis [Evidence: B]
- DIM (200mg) — estrogen metabolism [Evidence: C+]
- Berberine (500mg) — insulin sensitivity [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Low glycemic diet (reduce insulin spikes)
- Elimination trial: dairy, high-glycemic foods
- Stress management (meditation, exercise)
- Sleep 7-9 hours (cortisol regulation)
- Regular exercise (insulin sensitivity)

**Professional:**
- Hormonal testing (androgens, DHEA-S, cortisol)
- Gut testing (SIBO breath test, stool analysis)
- Prescription: spironolactone, isotretinoin (severe), birth control

## 2. PHOTOAGING (Wrinkles, Fine Lines, Sun Damage)

### Primary Root Causes
- **UV-induced collagen degradation** (MMP activation)
- **Oxidative stress** (free radical damage)
- **Glycation** (sugar-protein cross-linking)
- **Cellular senescence** (telomere shortening)
- **Hormonal decline** (estrogen loss in perimenopause)

### Contributing Factors
- Chronic sun exposure (UVA > UVB for aging)
- Smoking (accelerates aging by 10 years)
- Poor nutrition (antioxidant deficiency)
- Dehydration
- Repetitive facial expressions
- Sleep position (compression wrinkles)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Deep wrinkles with significant volume loss
- Precancerous lesions (actinic keratosis)
- Rapid onset (possible other cause)

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Retinoids (tretinoin 0.025-0.1% or retinol 0.3-1%) — collagen synthesis [Evidence: A]
- Vitamin C (15-20% L-ascorbic acid) — antioxidant, collagen [Evidence: A]
- Niacinamide (5%) — barrier repair, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B+]
- Peptides (matrixyl, copper) — collagen stimulation [Evidence: B]
- AHAs (glycolic acid 8-10%) — cell turnover [Evidence: B+]
- Sunscreen SPF 30+ (zinc oxide/titanium dioxide) — prevention [Evidence: A+]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Collagen peptides (10g) — skin elasticity [Evidence: B]
- Vitamin C (1g) — collagen synthesis [Evidence: B]
- CoQ10 (100mg) — mitochondrial support [Evidence: B]
- Polypodium leucotomos (240mg) — oral photoprotection [Evidence: B]
- Omega-3 (2g) — membrane integrity [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Strict sun protection (primary prevention)
- Quit smoking (biggest single improvement)
- Adequate hydration
- Sleep on back (reduce compression)
- Manage stress (cortisol accelerates aging)
- Limit sugar (reduce glycation)

**Professional:**
- Botox (dynamic wrinkles)
- Dermal fillers (volume loss)
- Laser resurfacing (CO2, Fraxel)
- Microneedling (collagen induction)
- Chemical peels (TCA, phenol)

## 3. HYPERPIGMENTATION (Melasma, PIH, Sun Spots)

### Primary Root Causes
- **UV exposure** (melanocyte stimulation)
- **Hormonal changes** (melasma: estrogen, progesterone)
- **Inflammation** (PIH: post-inflammatory)
- **Oxidative stress**
- **Insulin resistance** (acanthosis nigricans)

### Contributing Factors
- Pregnancy (melasma)
- Birth control pills
- Hormone replacement therapy
- Skin trauma (picking, procedures)
- Heat exposure (infrared)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Rapidly changing or irregular borders
- Blue-gray color (dermal melasma — harder to treat)
- Associated with systemic symptoms

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Hydroquinone (2-4%) — tyrosinase inhibitor [Evidence: A]
- Vitamin C (15-20%) — antioxidant, brightening [Evidence: B+]
- Niacinamide (4-5%) — melanosome transfer inhibition [Evidence: B+]
- Azelaic acid (20%) — tyrosinase inhibitor [Evidence: B+]
- Kojic acid (1-4%) — tyrosinase inhibitor [Evidence: B]
- Retinoids — cell turnover [Evidence: B+]
- Alpha arbutin (1-2%) — tyrosinase inhibitor [Evidence: B]
- Tranexamic acid (2-5%) — melanin synthesis inhibition [Evidence: B+]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Polypodium leucotomos (240mg) — oral photoprotection [Evidence: B]
- Glutathione (500mg) — antioxidant, melanin regulation [Evidence: C+]
- Vitamin C (1g) — antioxidant [Evidence: B]
- Pycnogenol (100mg) — antioxidant, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Strict sun protection (non-negotiable)
- Avoid heat exposure (saunas, hot yoga worsen melasma)
- Manage hormones (if on HBC, discuss alternatives)
- Don't pick at skin (prevents PIH)

**Professional:**
- Chemical peels (Glycolic, TCA, Jessner)
- Laser (Q-switched Nd:YAG, PicoSure — careful with melasma)
- Microneedling (with tranexamic acid)
- Prescription: triple therapy (HQ + tretinoin + steroid)

## 4. ROSACEA

### Primary Root Causes
- **Neurovascular dysregulation** (abnormal blood vessel response)
- **Demodex mite overgrowth** (folliculorum, brevis)
- **Barrier dysfunction** (impaired skin barrier)
- **Inflammation** (innate immune activation)
- **Gut dysbiosis** (SIBO association, 50-70%)

### Contributing Factors
- Heat/sun exposure
- Alcohol (vasodilation)
- Spicy foods
- Stress (flushing)
- Steroid use (topical or systemic)
- H. pylori infection (some evidence)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Eye involvement (ocular rosacea)
- Rhinophyma (nose thickening)
- No improvement after 8 weeks
- Severe flushing affecting quality of life

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Azelaic acid (15-20%) — anti-inflammatory, anti-mite [Evidence: A]
- Metronidazole (0.75-1%) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: A]
- Ivermectin (1%) — anti-Demodex [Evidence: A]
- Niacinamide (4-5%) — barrier repair [Evidence: B+]
- Sulfur (5-10%) — anti-mite, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Green tea extract — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Zinc (30mg) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Probiotics (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium) — gut-skin axis [Evidence: B]
- Digestive enzymes — support gut health [Evidence: C+]

**Lifestyle:**
- Identify and avoid triggers (keep diary)
- Manage stress (flushing)
- Gentle skincare (no scrubs, no acids initially)
- Cool compresses for flushing
- Sun protection (physical sunscreen)

**Professional:**
- Prescription: doxycycline (anti-inflammatory dose), ivermectin
- Laser (pulsed dye, Nd:YAG for vessels)
- Treat SIBO if present (breath test)
- Avoid steroids (worsens rosacea)

## 5. ECZEMA / ATOPIC DERMATITIS

### Primary Root Causes
- **Barrier dysfunction** (filaggrin mutations, ceramide deficiency)
- **Immune dysregulation** (Th2 dominant response)
- **Microbiome imbalance** (S. aureus overgrowth, reduced diversity)
- **Allergic sensitization** (IgE-mediated)

### Contributing Factors
- Dry environment
- Irritants (fragrance, detergents, wool)
- Stress (flares)
- Food allergies (children more than adults)
- Hard water
- Temperature extremes

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Widespread (>20% body surface)
- Significant sleep disruption
- Signs of infection (weeping, crusting)
- No response to topical steroids
- Eczema herpeticum (medical emergency)

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Ceramides — barrier repair [Evidence: A]
- Colloidal oatmeal — anti-inflammatory, barrier [Evidence: B]
- Niacinamide (2-4%) — barrier repair [Evidence: B+]
- Humectants (glycerin, hyaluronic acid, urea) — hydration [Evidence: B]
- Mild corticosteroids (hydrocortisone 1%) — acute flares [Evidence: A]
- Calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, pimecrolimus) — steroid-sparing [Evidence: A]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Probiotics (Lactobacillus rhamnosus GG) — immune modulation [Evidence: B]
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Vitamin D (2000 IU) — immune regulation [Evidence: B]
- Quercetin (500mg) — mast cell stabilization [Evidence: C+]

**Lifestyle:**
- Lukewarm baths/showers (not hot)
- Fragrance-free everything
- Humidifier in bedroom
- Cotton clothing (avoid wool/synthetics)
- Stress management (flares)
- Wet wrap therapy (severe flares)
- Patch testing for allergens

**Professional:**
- Prescription: topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, JAK inhibitors
- Phototherapy (NB-UVB)
- Dupilumab (Dupixent) — biologic for severe
- Allergy testing
- Infection management

## 6. DRY / DEHYDRATED SKIN

### Primary Root Causes
- **Barrier dysfunction** (ceramide deficiency, filaggrin)
- **Low environmental humidity**
- **Aging** (decreased sebum, HA, collagen)
- **Harsh skincare** (over-cleansing, stripping products)
- **Dehydration** (internal fluid loss)
- **Thyroid dysfunction** (hypothyroidism)

### Contributing Factors
- Hot showers
- Harsh detergents
- Retinoids (without proper moisturization)
- AHAs/BHAs (overuse)
- Medications (diuretics, isotretinoin)
- Climate (cold, dry, windy)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Cracking/fissuring
- Signs of infection
- Associated with systemic symptoms (thyroid)
- No improvement with moisturization

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Ceramides — barrier repair [Evidence: A]
- Hyaluronic acid — hydration [Evidence: B]
- Glycerin — humectant [Evidence: B]
- Squalane — emollient [Evidence: B]
- Urea (5-10%) — humectant + mild exfoliant [Evidence: B]
- Niacinamide (2-5%) — barrier repair [Evidence: B+]
- Occlusives (petrolatum, dimethicone) — prevent TEWL [Evidence: A]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — membrane fluidity [Evidence: B]
- Hyaluronic acid (120mg) — hydration [Evidence: B]
- Vitamin E (400 IU) — antioxidant, barrier [Evidence: B]
- Collagen peptides (10g) — skin structure [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Lukewarm showers (not hot)
- Gentle cleanser (pH 5.5)
- Humidifier
- Increase water intake
- Avoid alcohol (dehydrating)
- Protect from wind/cold

**Professional:**
- Prescription: urea 20-40% (severe dryness)
- Treat underlying thyroid if present
- In-office hydration treatments

## 7. SENSITIVE SKIN

### Primary Root Causes
- **Barrier dysfunction** (impaired lipid matrix)
- **Nerve hypersensitivity** (increased TRPV1 expression)
- **Microbiome imbalance** (reduced diversity)
- **Inflammation** (subclinical)

### Contributing Factors
- Over-exfoliation
- Harsh products (SLS, alcohol, fragrance)
- Environmental stressors (pollution, UV)
- Stress (neuropeptide release)
- Temperature extremes

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Persistent redness with burning
- Reaction to many products
- Associated with other conditions (rosacea, eczema)

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Niacinamide (2-4%) — barrier repair, anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B+]
- Ceramides — barrier repair [Evidence: A]
- Centella asiatica — soothing, healing [Evidence: B]
- Allantoin — soothing [Evidence: B]
- Bisabolol — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Thermal spring water — soothing [Evidence: B]
- Minimal ingredients (avoid fragrance, essential oils, alcohol)

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Probiotics — gut-skin axis [Evidence: B]
- Magnesium (400mg) — nerve function [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Gentle, minimal routine
- Patch test new products
- Avoid fragrance, essential oils
- Manage stress
- Protect from environmental stressors

**Professional:**
- Patch testing for allergens
- Treat underlying condition (rosacea, eczema)
- Prescription: barrier repair creams

## 8. OILY SKIN / ENLARGED PORES

### Primary Root Causes
- **Androgen stimulation** (sebaceous gland activity)
- **Genetics** (sebaceous gland size/number)
- **Insulin resistance** (IGF-1 increases sebum)
- **Dehydration** (compensatory sebum production)

### Contributing Factors
- Humidity
- Hormonal fluctuations (menstrual cycle)
- Stress (cortisol)
- Over-cleansing (rebound effect)
- Dairy consumption (IGF-1)
- High glycemic diet

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist If:
- Sudden onset (possible hormonal disorder)
- Very severe oiliness
- Associated with acne, hirsutism (PCOS)

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Niacinamide (4-5%) — sebum regulation [Evidence: B+]
- Salicylic acid (2%) — unclogs pores [Evidence: A]
- Retinoids — cell turnover, pore size [Evidence: A]
- Clay masks (kaolin, bentonite) — absorb oil [Evidence: B]
- Green tea extract — sebum reduction [Evidence: B]
- Lightweight, non-comedogenic moisturizers

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Zinc (30mg) — sebum regulation [Evidence: B]
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — anti-inflammatory [Evidence: B]
- Berberine (500mg) — insulin sensitivity [Evidence: B]
- DIM (200mg) — estrogen metabolism [Evidence: C+]

**Lifestyle:**
- Low glycemic diet
- Limit dairy
- Manage stress
- Don't over-cleanse (stripping causes rebound)
- Blotting papers (temporary)

**Professional:**
- Prescription: spironolactone (hormonal), isotretinoin (severe)
- Laser (sebaceous gland reduction)
- Chemical peels

## 9. DARK CIRCLES / PERIORBITAL HYPERPIGMENTATION

### Primary Root Causes
- **Genetics** (familial periorbital melanosis)
- **Vascular congestion** (leaky capillaries, hemosiderin)
- **Volume loss** (tear trough, orbital fat loss)
- **Allergies** (allergic shiner)
- **Sleep deprivation** (venous pooling)
- **Aging** (thinning skin shows vessels)

### Contributing Factors
- Eye rubbing
- Sun exposure
- Dehydration
- Anemia
- Nasal congestion
- Screen time (eye strain)

### Severity Indicators — See Dermatologist / Oculoplastic Surgeon If:
- Sudden onset (possible systemic cause)
- Associated with swelling/pain
- Significant volume loss
- Doesn't improve with sleep/treatment

### Holistic Treatment
**Products (Topical):**
- Vitamin C (10-15%) — brightening, antioxidant [Evidence: B]
- Vitamin K — vascular health [Evidence: C]
- Caffeine — vasoconstriction [Evidence: B]
- Retinoids — collagen, thickening [Evidence: B]
- Peptides — collagen stimulation [Evidence: B]
- Hyaluronic acid — hydration [Evidence: B]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Iron (if anemic) [Evidence: A if deficient]
- Vitamin K2 — vascular health [Evidence: B]
- Quercetin — vascular support [Evidence: C+]
- Adequate sleep (7-9 hours)

**Lifestyle:**
- Sleep elevated (reduce pooling)
- Cold compresses (vasoconstriction)
- Manage allergies
- Reduce screen time
- UV protection (sunglasses)
- Don't rub eyes

**Professional:**
- Dermal fillers (tear trough)
- Laser (vascular, pigment)
- Chemical peels
- Prescription: hydroquinone (careful near eye)

- BHAs (salicylic acid 2%) — pore penetration [Evidence: A]
- Retinoids — cell turnover [Evidence: A]

- Physical exfoliation (gentle) — sloughing [Evidence: B]

**Supplements (Internal):**
- Omega-3 (2g EPA+DHA) — cell membrane [Evidence: B]
- Vitamin A — cell turnover [Evidence: B]
- Zinc — healing [Evidence: B]

**Lifestyle:**
- Regular exfoliation (not over-exfoliation)
- Hydration
- Sun protection
- Humidifier

**Professional:**
- Microdermabrasion
- Chemical peels
- Microneedling
- Laser resurfacing

## Severity Grading Scale (All Conditions)

| Grade | Description | Action |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| **Mild** (1-3) | Barely noticeable, occasional | Self-treatment acceptable |
| **Moderate** (4-6) | Noticeable, frequent | Self-treatment + monitor |
| **Severe** (7-8) | Significant, constant | **See dermatologist** |
| **Very Severe** (9-10) | Severe impact on life | **Urgent dermatologist** |

### When to Always See a Dermatologist
- Rapid change in any lesion
- Bleeding, crusting, non-healing
- Associated with systemic symptoms
- Significant emotional impact
- No improvement after 8-12 weeks of treatment
- Signs of infection

## Content Production Guidelines

### Writing Standards
- **Lead with benefit**, not mechanism ("This helps your skin stay firm" vs. "This inhibits MMP-1")
- **Use plain language** — explain terms in parentheses on first use
- **Include specific numbers** — "Studies show 15% vitamin C reduces fine lines by 23%"
- **Acknowledge uncertainty** — "Early research suggests... more studies needed"
- **Avoid absolute claims** — "may help" not "will cure"
- **Include realistic timelines** — no overnight miracles

### Evidence Grading
Every article must include:
- 🔷 Strong (multiple RCTs)
- 🔶 Moderate (limited RCTs + mechanistic evidence)
- 🟢 Emerging (preclinical + early data)
- ⚪ Limited (anecdotal/theoretical)

### Source Requirements
- Minimum 3 peer-reviewed sources for 🔷 articles
- Minimum 1 peer-reviewed source for all others
- Include DOI or PubMed link where possible
- Date sources (preferably within 5 years)

### Visual Requirements
- Header image: relevant, diverse, age-appropriate
- Infographics: ingredient comparisons, routine flowcharts
- Photo examples: before/after (with consent, standardized lighting)
- Product images: neutral backgrounds, clear labels

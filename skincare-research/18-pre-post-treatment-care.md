## Injectables

### Pre-Treatment Preparation (24-48 Hours Before)
- **Avoid blood thinners**: Discontinue aspirin, ibuprofen, naproxen, vitamin E, fish oil, and herbal supplements (gingko, garlic, ginseng) for at least 48 hours prior to treatment
- **Alcohol avoidance**: Refrain from alcohol consumption for 24 hours before treatment to minimize bruising risk
- **Hydration**: Increase water intake to 8-10 glasses daily starting 2 days before treatment
- **Skin preparation**: Arrive with clean, makeup-free skin in the treatment area
- **Medical history review**: Disclose all medications, allergies, and medical conditions to your provider
- **Pregnancy/nursing**: Inform provider if pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding

### Immediate Post-Treatment Care (First 24 Hours)
- **Cold compresses**: Apply intermittently (10 minutes on, 10 minutes off) to reduce swelling and discomfort
- **Head elevation**: Keep head elevated while sleeping to minimize swelling
- **Avoid touching**: Refrain from touching, rubbing, or applying pressure to treated areas
- **Makeup avoidance**: Wait at least 4 hours before applying makeup to treated areas
- **Exercise restriction**: Avoid strenuous exercise, saunas, hot tubs, and excessive heat for 24 hours
- **Alcohol avoidance**: Refrain from alcohol for 24 hours post-treatment
- **Medication continuation**: Resume normal medications unless otherwise directed by provider

### Days 2-7 Post-Treatment
- **Gentle cleansing**: Use mild, fragrance-free cleanser twice daily
- **Moisturization**: Apply fragrance-free moisturizer to maintain skin barrier integrity
- **Sun protection**: Use broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily, reapplying every 2 hours when outdoors
- **Activity resumption**: Gradually resume normal activities as tolerated
- **Follow-up assessment**: Schedule follow-up appointment as recommended by provider (typically 2 weeks)

### Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)
- **Consistent skincare**: Maintain daily cleansing, moisturizing, and sun protection routine
- **Touch-up scheduling**: Plan maintenance treatments based on product longevity (typically every 3-6 months for neuromodulators, 6-18 months for fillers)
- **Lifestyle factors**: Maintain healthy diet, adequate hydration, and consistent sleep patterns
- **Annual reassessment**: Schedule annual comprehensive skin assessment with provider

### What to Avoid
- **Immediate post-treatment (first 6 hours)**: Lying flat, vigorous exercise, excessive heat exposure
- **First 24 hours**: Alcohol consumption, blood-thinning medications/supplements, facial massage
- **First week**: Aggressive skincare products (retinoids, acids, scrubs) in treated areas
- **Ongoing**: Excessive sun exposure without protection, smoking

## Laser Treatments

### Pre-Treatment Preparation (2-4 Weeks Before)
- **Sun avoidance**: Strict avoidance of direct sun exposure and tanning beds for minimum 2 weeks prior
- **Skin lightening agents**: If treating hyperpigmentation, may be prescribed hydroquinone or other lightening agents 2-4 weeks pre-treatment
- **Retinoid discontinuation**: Stop tretinoin, retinol, and other retinoids 3-5 days before treatment
- **Photosensitizing medications**: Review with provider; may need to discontinue certain antibiotics, antifungals, or isotretinoin
- **Herpes prophylaxis**: For lip/perioral treatments, antiviral medication may be prescribed if history of cold sores
- **Skin preparation**: Discontinue use of irritating products (alcohol-based toners, astringents) 3-5 days pre-treatment
- **Hydration protocol**: Increase water intake and use hydrating serums containing hyaluronic acid

### Immediate Post-Treatment Care (First 24 Hours)
- **Cooling protocols**: Use cold compresses or cooling gels as directed by provider
- **Cleansing**: Gentle cleansing with lukewarm water only; avoid rubbing or scrubbing
- **Moisturization**: Apply recommended post-laser moisturizer frequently (every 2-3 hours while awake)
- **Makeup avoidance**: Typically avoided for 24-48 hours depending on treatment intensity
- **Activity restrictions**: Avoid strenuous exercise, swimming, saunas, and hot tubs
- **Sun protection**: Absolute avoidance of direct sun exposure; wear wide-brimmed hat if outdoor exposure is unavoidable

### Days 2-7 Post-Treatment (Healing Phase)
- **Cleansing routine**: Gentle cleansing twice daily with recommended cleanser
- **Moisturization**: Continue frequent application of healing moisturizers
- **Makeup reintroduction**: May resume mineral-based makeup after 48 hours if no crusting or oozing
- **Sun protection**: Non-negotiable broad-spectrum SPF 50+ reapplied every 2 hours
- **Activity progression**: Light exercise permitted after 48 hours if no discomfort
- **Product restrictions**: Avoid retinoids, acids, vitamin C serums, and exfoliants until fully healed

### Weeks 2-4 Post-Treatment (Recovery Phase)
- **Hyperpigmentation management**: May experience temporary darkening; continue sun protection and consider prescribed lightening agents
- **Texture improvement**: Gradual improvement in skin texture as collagen remodeling occurs
- **Product reintroduction**: Gradually reintroduce active skincare products as tolerated
- **Follow-up treatments**: Schedule maintenance sessions as recommended by provider
- **Scar monitoring**: Monitor for any signs of hypertrophic scarring or keloid formation

### Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)
- **Sun protection vigilance**: Daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+ minimum, SPF 50+ for extended outdoor exposure
- **Antioxidant incorporation**: Daily use of vitamin C serum in morning routine
- **Retinoid maintenance**: Nightly retinoid use as tolerated for ongoing collagen stimulation
- **Hydration focus**: Maintain optimal skin hydration with appropriate moisturizers
- **Annual laser assessment**: Yearly evaluation to determine maintenance treatment needs

### What to Avoid
- **Immediate post-treatment**: Heat exposure (hot showers, saunas, steam rooms), direct sunlight
- **First week**: Picking at crusts or scabs, aggressive skincare, waxing in treated areas
- **First 2 weeks**: Swimming in chlorinated pools, excessive sweating activities
- **Ongoing**: Inconsistent sun protection, smoking, excessive alcohol consumption

## Chemical Peels

### Pre-Treatment Preparation (1-2 Weeks Before)
- **Retinoid discontinuation**: Stop tretinoin, retinol, adapalene, and tazarotene 3-5 days before peel
- **Exfoliant avoidance**: Discontinue use of AHAs, BHAs, physical scrubs, and enzymes 3-5 days pre-treatment
- **Skin priming**: May be prescribed pre-peel regimen (hydroquinone, tretinoin) for 2-4 weeks for deeper peels
- **Antiviral prophylaxis**: For those with herpes history, antiviral medication may be prescribed
- **Hydration enhancement**: Increase use of hydrating serums and moisturizers
- **Sun protection diligence**: Strict broad-spectrum SPF 30+ use for minimum 1 week pre-treatment
- **Avoid irritants**: Discontinue use of alcohol-based products, fragrances, and essential oils

### Immediate Post-Treatment Care (First 24 Hours)
- **Sensation management**: Expected tingling, warmth, or tightness; use prescribed soothing products
- **Cleansing protocol**: Gentle cleansing with cool water and recommended neutral cleanser
- **Moisturization**: Frequent application of barrier-repair moisturizer (every 1-2 hours while awake)
- **Makeup avoidance**: Typically avoided for 24 hours for superficial peels, 48-72 hours for medium/deep peels
- **Activity restrictions**: Avoid exercise, heat exposure, and excessive facial movement
- **Sun protection**: Complete avoidance of direct sun exposure

### Days 2-7 Post-Treatment (Peeling Phase)
- **Cleansing continuation**: Gentle cleansing twice daily; pat dry, do not rub
- **Moisturization protocol**: Continue frequent application of healing moisturizers
- **Peeling management**: Allow natural exfoliation; DO NOT pick, pull, or forcibly remove peeling skin
- **Makeup reintroduction**: Mineral makeup may be used once significant peeling subsides (typically day 4-5 for superficial peels)
- **Sun protection**: Non-negotiable broad-spectrum SPF 50+; physical blockers (zinc oxide/titanium dioxide) preferred
- **Activity progression**: Light walking permitted; avoid activities causing excessive sweating or heat

### Weeks 2-4 Post-Treatment (Recovery Phase)
- **Hyperpigmentation monitoring**: Watch for post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation; continue aggressive sun protection
- **Texture normalization**: Skin texture continues to improve as new epidermal layer matures
- **Product reintroduction**: Gradually reintroduce active ingredients as tolerated (start with lowest concentrations)
- **Follow-up assessment**: Evaluate results and plan maintenance peel schedule
- **Hydration maintenance**: Continue focus on barrier repair and hydration

### Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)
- **Sun protection paramount**: Daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+; reapplication every 2 hours outdoors
- **Antioxidant protection**: Daily vitamin C serum to prevent oxidative damage
- **Gentle exfoliation maintenance**: 1-2 times weekly with low-concentration acids or enzymes
- **Moisture barrier support**: Ceramide-rich moisturizers to maintain skin barrier integrity
- **Peel maintenance schedule**: Superficial peels every 4-6 weeks; medium peels every 3-4 months; deep peels as clinically indicated (typically 1+ years apart)

### What to Avoid
- **Immediate post-treatment**: Water temperature extremes (very hot or cold), rubbing or scrubbing skin
- **First 3-5 days**: Picking at peeling skin, using retinols or acids, facial waxing/threading
- **First week**: Swimming pools, hot tubs, saunas, excessive sweating
- **First 2 weeks**: Direct sun exposure without protection, tanning beds
- **Ongoing**: Inconsistent sun protection, smoking, harsh physical exfoliants

## Microneedling

### Pre-Treatment Preparation (3-5 Days Before)
- **Retinoid discontinuation**: Stop tretinoin, retinol, and other retinoids 3-5 days before treatment
- **Exfoliant avoidance**: Discontinue AHAs, BHAs, and physical exfoliants 3 days pre-treatment
- **Antiviral consideration**: For those with herpes history, discuss prophylactic antiviral with provider
- **Blood thinner review**: Discuss aspirin, NSAIDs, and supplements with provider; may need temporary discontinuation
- **Skin hydration**: Increase use of hydrating serums containing hyaluronic acid
- **Sun protection**: Maintain consistent broad-spectrum SPF 30+ use
- **Avoid irritants**: Discontinue use of potentially irritating products (alcohol, fragrances, essential oils)

### Immediate Post-Treatment Care (First 24 Hours)
- **Expected sensations**: Mild to moderate warmth, tightness, and slight sunburn-like feeling
- **Cleansing protocol**: Gentle cleansing with lukewarm water only after 6-8 hours post-treatment
- **Topical avoidance**: Avoid applying any products for first 6-8 hours unless specifically directed by provider
- **Cooling measures**: Cool compresses may provide comfort if experiencing significant warmth
- **Makeup avoidance**: Typically avoided for 24 hours post-treatment
- **Activity restrictions**: Avoid exercise, saunas, steam rooms, and excessive heat exposure
- **Touch avoidance**: Refrain from touching treated areas with unwashed hands

### Days 2-7 Post-Treatment (Healing Phase)
- **Cleansing routine**: Gentle cleansing twice daily with recommended pH-balanced cleanser
- **Hydration focus**: Apply hyaluronic acid serum followed by barrier-repair moisturizer
- **Makeup reintroduction**: Mineral makeup may be used after 24 hours if no excessive redness or irritation
- **Sun protection**: Broad-spectrum SPF 30+ minimum; SPF 50+ recommended for outdoor activities
- **Activity progression**: Light exercise permitted after 48 hours; avoid activities causing excessive facial sweating
- **Product restrictions**: Avoid retinoids, acids, vitamin C serums, and exfoliants until fully healed (typically 5-7 days)

### Weeks 2-4 Post-Treatment (Recovery Phase)
- **Collagen stimulation**: Peak collagen production occurs around week 2-4 post-treatment
- **Texture improvement**: Continued improvement in skin texture, pore appearance, and fine lines
- **Product reintroduction**: Gradually reintroduce active skincare products as tolerated
- **Treatment scheduling**: Discuss maintenance microneedling schedule with provider (typically every 4-6 weeks for initial series)
- **Combination therapies**: May consider combining with PRP, vitamin C, or growth factor serums for enhanced results

### Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)
- **Consistent microneedling**: Maintenance treatments every 4-8 weeks for optimal collagen stimulation
- **Sun protection vigilance**: Daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+; critical for protecting newly stimulated collagen
- **Antioxidant incorporation**: Daily vitamin C serum in morning routine to support collagen synthesis
- **Hydration maintenance**: Hyaluronic acid and ceramide-containing moisturizers to maintain barrier function
- **Retinoid integration**: Gradual reintroduction of retinoids 2-3 times weekly as tolerated for ongoing benefits

### What to Avoid
- **Immediate post-treatment**: Touching face with unwashed hands, applying makeup or skincare too soon
- **First 24 hours**: Exercise, saunas, steam rooms, excessive heat exposure
- **First 3-5 days**: Retinoids, acids, vitamin C serums, exfoliants, facial waxing
- **First week**: Swimming pools, hot tubs, direct sun exposure without protection
- **Ongoing**: Inconsistent sun protection, smoking, aggressive physical exfoliants

## Biostimulators (Sculptra, Radiesse, Bellafill, etc.)

### Pre-Treatment Preparation (1-2 Weeks Before)
- **Blood thinner management**: Discuss with provider; may need to temporarily discontinue aspirin, NSAIDs, blood thinners, and supplements
- **Alcohol moderation**: Limit alcohol consumption for 48 hours before treatment
- **Smoking cessation**: Strongly recommended to stop smoking for at least 2 weeks before and after treatment
- **Hydration optimization**: Increase water intake to support optimal tissue response
- **Skin preparation**: Arrive with clean, makeup-free skin in treatment areas
- **Medical history disclosure**: Inform provider of autoimmune conditions, bleeding disorders, or history of keloid formation
- **Allergy assessment**: Discuss any known allergies to treatment components (particularly important for bovine collagen in Radiesse)

### Immediate Post-Treatment Care (First 24 Hours)
- **Massage protocol**: Critical for certain biostimulators (Sculptra): 5 minutes of massage, 5 times daily, for 5 days post-treatment
- **Cold compresses**: Apply intermittently to reduce swelling and discomfort
- **Head elevation**: Sleep with head elevated to minimize swelling
- **Makeup avoidance**: Typically avoided for 4-6 hours post-treatment
- **Activity restrictions**: Avoid strenuous exercise, saunas, hot tubs, and excessive heat for 24 hours
- **Alcohol avoidance**: Refrain from alcohol for 24 hours post-treatment
- **Medication review**: Resume medications as directed by provider

### Days 2-7 Post-Treatment
- **Continued massage**: For Sculptra, continue 5x5x5 massage regimen through day 5
- **Gentle cleansing**: Use mild, fragrance-free cleanser twice daily
- **Moisturization**: Apply recommended moisturizer to maintain skin barrier
- **Sun protection**: Broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily; critical for protecting investment in collagen stimulation
- **Activity resumption**: Gradually resume normal activities as tolerated
- **Follow-up scheduling**: Schedule follow-up appointment to assess results and plan additional sessions

### Weeks 2-4 Post-Treatment
- **Massage completion**: Complete 5x5x5 regimen for Sculptra by day 5
- **Early results assessment**: Initial volumizing effects visible; collagen stimulation begins
- **Texture improvement**: Gradual improvement in skin quality and firmness
- **Hydration focus**: Continue optimal hydration to support collagen synthesis
- **Sun protection diligence**: Non-negotiable daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+
- **Lifestyle factors**: Maintain healthy diet, adequate sleep, and stress management

### Months 2-3 Post-Treatment
- **Peak collagen stimulation**: Maximum collagen production typically occurs around 2-3 months post-treatment
- **Results evaluation**: Assess volumizing effects and skin quality improvements
- **Treatment planning**: Discuss need for additional sessions based on desired outcomes
- **Maintenance planning**: Begin considering long-term maintenance schedule

### Long-Term Maintenance (Ongoing)
- **Sun protection paramount**: Daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+ minimum; UV exposure degrades collagen
- **Antioxidant protection**: Daily vitamin C serum to prevent oxidative damage to new collagen
- **Hydration focus**: Optimal hydration supports collagen integrity and skin plumpness
- **Healthy lifestyle**: Balanced diet rich in vitamin C, amino acids, and antioxidants supports collagen maintenance
- **Maintenance treatments**: Schedule based on specific biostimulator longevity (Sculptra: 2+ years; Radiesse: 12-18 months; Bellafill: 5+ years)
- **Annual reassessment**: Comprehensive evaluation to determine ongoing treatment needs

### What to Avoid
- **Immediate post-treatment**: Lying flat (first 4 hours for Sculptra), vigorous exercise, excessive heat
- **First 24 hours**: Alcohol consumption, blood-thinning medications/supplements without provider approval
- **First week**: Aggressive facial massage (except prescribed regimen), facial treatments that manipulate tissue
- **First 2 weeks**: Sun exposure without protection, smoking
- **Ongoing**: Inconsistent sun protection, smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, extreme weight fluctuations

## General Guidelines Across All Treatments

### Communication with Provider
- **Report concerns promptly**: Excessive pain, signs of infection, unusual swelling, or persistent symptoms
- **Follow-up adherence**: Attend all scheduled follow-up appointments
- **Honesty about compliance**: Be transparent about following (or not following) aftercare instructions
- **Question asking**: Never hesitate to ask questions about expected recovery or concerns

### Documentation and Tracking
- **Photographic documentation**: Consider before/after photos to track progress objectively
- **Symptom journal**: Note any unusual sensations or changes during recovery period
- **Product tracking**: Document skincare products used during recovery for future reference
- **Treatment records**: Maintain personal record of all treatments received for provider reference

### When to Seek Medical Attention
- **Signs of infection**: Increasing pain, warmth, redness, swelling, or purulent discharge
- **Allergic reactions**: Hives, difficulty breathing, swelling of face/lips/tongue
- **Persistent symptoms**: Numbness, weakness, vision changes, or severe headache lasting >24 hours
- **Unusual asymmetry**: Significant unevenness that worsens over time
- **Vascular compromise**: Skin discoloration (bluish/purple), severe pain, or delayed capillary refill

### Lifestyle Factors for Optimal Results
- **Nutrition**: Diet rich in antioxidants, vitamins A/C/E, omega-3 fatty acids, and lean proteins
- **Hydration**: Minimum 8 glasses water daily; more with exercise or in dry climates
- **Sleep**: 7-9 hours quality sleep nightly for optimal cellular repair
- **Stress management**: Chronic stress accelerates aging; incorporate relaxation techniques
- **Exercise**: Regular moderate exercise improves circulation and skin health
- **Smoking cessation**: Smoking significantly impairs healing and accelerates aging
- **Alcohol moderation**: Excessive alcohol dehydrates skin and impairs healing

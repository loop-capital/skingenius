# SKINgenius: Root Cause to Intervention Mapping

> **Purpose:** Map internal health root causes → skin manifestations → evidence-based interventions (topical + internal + lifestyle)
> **Evidence Grades:** A (strong RCT/meta-analysis), B (moderate evidence), C (limited/small trials), D (expert consensus/preclinical)

---

## 1. GUT-SKIN AXIS

### 1.1 Gut Dysbiosis → Skin Conditions

**Mechanism:** Disruption of gut microbiome composition alters immune signaling (Th17/Treg balance), increases systemic inflammation, and affects metabolite production (SCFAs, neurotransmitters).

| Root Cause | Skin Manifestation | Pathway | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gut dysbiosis | Acne vulgaris | Systemic LPS → inflammation; C. acnes overgrowth | B |
| Gut dysbiosis | Atopic dermatitis | Treg reduction → Th2 skewing; IgE sensitization | A |
| Gut dysbiosis | Psoriasis | Th17 activation; bacterial metabolite changes | B |
| Gut dysbiosis | Rosacea | SIBO association; low stomach acid | B |
| Gut dysbiosis | Seborrheic dermatitis | Malassezia interaction with gut flora | C |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** None direct; maintain barrier (ceramides, niacinamide)
- **Internal:** 
  - Probiotics: *Lactobacillus rhamnosus* GG (LGG), *Lactobacillus helveticus*, *Bifidobacterium longum* — 10^9-10^10 CFU/day [A]
  - Prebiotics: Inulin, FOS, GOS — 5-10g/day [B]
  - Postbiotics: SCFA supplementation (butyrate 300-500mg) [C]
  - Oral azelaic acid for rosacea (if SIBO-related) [B]
- **Lifestyle:** Fiber-rich diet (30g+/day), fermented foods, avoid unnecessary antibiotics
- **Professional:** Comprehensive stool analysis; SIBO breath testing for rosacea
- **When to see derm:** If skin condition fails to improve after 8-12 weeks of gut-focused intervention

### 1.2 Leaky Gut → Inflammation → Skin Impact

**Mechanism:** Intestinal hyperpermeability allows LPS, food antigens, and bacterial metabolites to enter bloodstream → systemic inflammation → cytokine-mediated skin damage.

| Skin Impact | Pathway | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Inflammatory acne | LPS → TLR4 activation → IL-1β, TNF-α | B |
| Eczema flares | Food antigen penetration → IgE/IgG | B |
| Psoriasis | Endotoxin → Th17 cascade | C |
| Accelerated aging | Chronic low-grade inflammation ("inflammaging") | B |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Anti-inflammatory ingredients (niacinamide 5%, green tea EGCG, centella asiatica) [B]
- **Internal:**
  - Zinc carnosine 75mg 2x/day (gut healing) [B]
  - L-glutamine 5g/day (intestinal repair) [C]
  - Curcumin 500-1000mg/day (anti-inflammatory) [B]
  - Vitamin D 2000-4000 IU/day (tight junction support) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Elimination diet (test gluten, dairy); stress reduction; adequate sleep
- **Professional:** zonulin testing; food sensitivity panels
- **When to see derm:** Persistent inflammatory skin + GI symptoms

### 1.3 SIBO → Rosacea Connection

**Mechanism:** Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth increases gut-derived inflammation; bacterial lipase activity may trigger skin flushing.

**Evidence:** 46-66% of rosacea patients test positive for SIBO (vs ~6-15% controls). Eradication improves rosacea in 70-80% of cases [B].

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** Rifaximin 550mg 3x/day x 10-14 days (if diagnosed) [A for SIBO; B for rosacea]
- **Topical:** Azelaic acid 15-20% (anti-inflammatory, anti-microbial) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Low-FODMAP diet during treatment; avoid trigger foods
- **Professional:** Hydrogen/methane breath testing; GI referral
- **When to see derm:** If SIBO treatment doesn't improve rosacea within 8 weeks

### 1.4 Microbiome-Specific Pathogens & Skin

#### C. acnes Phylotypes → Acne Severity
- **Phylotype IA1:** Most associated with inflammatory acne; produces CAMP factor, induces keratinocyte inflammation
- **Phylotype II:** More common in normal skin; less virulent
- **Phylotype III:** Associated with deep nodules

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Benzoyl peroxide 2.5-5% (broad antimicrobial, reduces resistance) [A]
- **Topical:** Clindamycin 1% (avoid as monotherapy; resistance concern) [A]
- **Internal:** Zinc gluconate 30mg/day (C. acnes inhibition) [B]
- **Internal:** Probiotics (see 1.1); oral lactoferrin 200mg/day [B]
- **Professional:** Culture/sensitivity if resistant; consider isotretinoin for severe

#### S. aureus → Atopic Dermatitis
- **Mechanism:** Colonization of lesional skin → superantigen production → Th2 amplification; biofilm formation increases resistance
- 90% of chronic eczema lesions are S. aureus-positive (vs 5-30% normal skin)

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Diluted bleach baths (0.005% = 1/4 cup bleach in full tub) 2x/week [A]
- **Topical:** Mupirocin 2% ointment for nasal carriage [A]
- **Topical:** Honey-based dressings (Manuka UMF 10+) [C]
- **Internal:** Probiotics (LGG specifically) [B]
- **Lifestyle:** Moisturize within 3 minutes of bathing; cotton clothing; avoid wool
- **Professional:** Nasal swab for carriage; MRSA screening if recurrent

#### Demodex Mites → Rosacea / Demodicosis
- **Mechanism:** *Demodex folliculorum* overpopulation triggers innate immune response; bacterial proteins on mite surface (Bacillus oleronius) activate immune cells

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Ivermectin 1% cream (Soolantra) [A]
- **Topical:** Permethrin 5% cream [B]
- **Topical:** Tea tree oil 50% (eyelid scrub for ocular demodicosis) [C]
- **Internal:** Ivermectin 200mcg/kg single dose (severe cases) [B]
- **Professional:** Skin scraping + microscopic exam; distinguish from papulopustular rosacea

#### Malassezia → Seborrheic Dermatitis
- **Mechanism:** Lipophilic yeast overgrowth in sebum-rich areas; oleic acid metabolism triggers inflammation

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Ketoconazole 2% shampoo/cream [A]
- **Topical:** Selenium sulfide 2.5% shampoo [A]
- **Topical:** Zinc pyrithione 1-2% shampoo [B]
- **Topical:** Ciclopirox olamine 1% [A]
- **Internal:** Itraconazole 200mg/day x 7 days (severe/recalcitrant) [B]
- **Internal:** Oral probiotics [C]
- **Lifestyle:** Regular shampooing; manage stress; cold weather protection

## 2. HORMONE-SKIN CONNECTION

### 2.1 Cortisol → Skin Impact

**Mechanism:** Chronic HPA axis activation elevates cortisol → multiple skin pathways.

| Pathway | Skin Manifestation | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen breakdown (MMP activation) | Accelerated aging, thinning | A |
| Sebum stimulation | Acne flares | B |
| Barrier disruption (ceramide reduction) | Sensitivity, dehydration | B |
| Immune suppression | Reactivation of HSV; delayed wound healing | A |
| Vasoconstriction → rebound | Redness, flushing | C |
| Telogen effluvium | Diffuse hair shedding | A |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Adaptogenic botanicals (ashwagandha extract in skincare) [C]
- **Topical:** Peptides (copper peptides, signal peptides) for collagen support [B]
- **Internal:**
  - Ashwagandha (KSM-66 or Sensoril) 300-600mg/day [B]
  - Phosphatidylserine 100-300mg/day (cortisol modulation) [B]
  - Magnesium glycinate 200-400mg (HPA axis support) [B]
  - Omega-3 EPA 2g+/day (anti-inflammatory counterbalance) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Sleep hygiene (7-9h); mindfulness/meditation (20min/day); nature exposure
- **Professional:** Salivary cortisol testing (4-point); consider adaptogenic IV therapy
- **When to see derm:** Sudden onset + systemic symptoms (weight gain, fatigue)

### 2.2 Estrogen Decline → Perimenopausal Skin Changes

**Mechanism:** Estrogen decline (starts ~age 35-40) → reduced fibroblast activity → collagen loss; decreased hyaluronic acid; reduced barrier lipids.

| Change | Timeline | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Collagen loss (30% in first 5 years post-menopause) | Perimenopause → post-menopause | A |
| Skin thinning (1-2%/year) | Gradual | A |
| Dryness | Immediate with estrogen drop | A |
| Decreased elasticity | 3-5 years post-menopause | A |
| Wrinkle deepening | Accelerated in perimenopause | A |
| Acne (hormonal shift) | Perimenopause paradox | B |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:**
  - Estriol 0.3% cream (prescription; bioidentical) [B]
  - Retinoids (tretinoin 0.025-0.1%) [A]
  - Hyaluronic acid (multi-molecular weight) [B]
  - Ceramides + cholesterol + fatty acids [A]
  - Growth factors [C]
- **Internal:**
  - Phytoestrogens: Isoflavones (genistein) 50mg/day [B]
  - Maca root 1500-3000mg/day [C]
  - Vitamin C 1000mg/day (collagen synthesis) [A]
  - Collagen peptides 10g/day [B]
  - HRT (consult physician; transdermal estradiol preferred) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Weight-bearing exercise (bone + skin); avoid smoking
- **Professional:** Hormone panel (estradiol, FSH, progesterone, testosterone)
- **When to see derm:** Rapid changes; consider derm-HRT collaborative care

### 2.3 Testosterone/Androgens → Sebum, Acne, Hirsutism

**Mechanism:** DHT binds androgen receptors in sebaceous glands → sebum overproduction; follicular hyperkeratinization.

| Condition | Hormonal Pattern | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Adult female acne | Elevated free testosterone, low SHBG | A |
| PCOS acne | Elevated androgens, insulin resistance | A |
| Menopausal acne | Relative androgen excess | B |
| Hirsutism | Elevated DHEA-S, testosterone | A |
| Seborrhea | DHT sensitivity in sebaceous glands | B |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:**
  - Spironolactone 1-5% cream (off-label; limited availability) [C]
  - Clascoterone 1% cream (Winlevi; topical androgen receptor blocker) [A]
  - Retinoids (tretinoin, adapalene) [A]
  - Salicylic acid 2% (follicular penetration) [B]
- **Internal:**
  - Spironolactone 50-200mg/day (anti-androgen; women only) [A]
  - Oral contraceptives (ethinyl estradiol + progestin) [A]
  - Saw palmetto 320mg/day (5-alpha reductase inhibition) [C]
  - Spearmint tea 2 cups/day (anti-androgen; hirsutism) [B]
  - Inositol (myo-inositol 2g + D-chiro-inositol 50mg) for PCOS [B]
- **Professional:** Hormone panel (total/free testosterone, DHEA-S, SHBG, 17-OH progesterone)
- **When to see derm:** Acne + irregular periods; sudden-onset hirsutism; need for spironolactone prescription

### 2.4 Insulin Resistance → Glycation, AGEs, Inflammation

**Mechanism:** Elevated insulin → IGF-1 stimulation → keratinocyte proliferation + sebum; chronic hyperglycemia → AGE formation → collagen crosslinking; inflammatory cytokine release.

| Skin Manifestation | Pathway | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Acne (all ages) | IGF-1 → sebocyte proliferation | A |
| Accelerated aging | AGEs → collagen stiffness, yellowing | A |
| Skin tags (acanthosis nigricans) | Insulin hyperstimulation | A |
| Poor wound healing | Microvascular damage, inflammation | A |
| Hidradenitis suppurativa | Strong insulin resistance link | A |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:**
  - Niacinamide 5% (improves insulin sensitivity in skin) [B]
  - Retinoids (counteract glycation) [B]
  - Carnosine 1% (anti-glycation) [C]
  - Aminoguanidine (research; anti-AGE) [D]
- **Internal:**
  - Berberine 500mg 2-3x/day (glucose-lowering comparable to metformin) [A]
  - Chromium picolinate 200-1000mcg/day [B]
  - Alpha-lipoic acid 300-600mg/day (insulin sensitization) [B]
  - Cinnamon extract 500mg/day [B]
  - Metformin 500-2000mg/day (if prescribed) [A]
  - Low-glycemic diet (GI <55) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Intermittent fasting (16:8); resistance training; post-meal walks
- **Professional:** Fasting insulin, HOMA-IR, HbA1c; consider endocrinology referral
- **When to see derm:** Acanthosis nigricans (may indicate undiagnosed diabetes); HS is high-insulin

### 2.5 Thyroid → Skin & Hair

**Mechanism:** Thyroid hormones regulate skin cell turnover, barrier function, and hair follicle cycling.

| Condition | Skin/Hair Manifestations | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Hypothyroidism | Dry, cool, pale skin; coarse hair; brittle nails; hair loss (outer 1/3 eyebrows) | A |
| Hyperthyroidism | Warm, moist skin; fine hair; pretibial myxedema (Graves) | A |
| Subclinical hypothyroidism | Subtle dryness, hair thinning | B |
| Hashimoto's | Dry skin + autoimmune inflammation | A |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Rich emollients (ceramides, shea butter, squalane); avoid harsh cleansers
- **Internal:**
  - Levothyroxine (if prescribed; gold standard) [A]
  - Selenium 200mcg/day (T4→T3 conversion, Hashimoto's) [B]
  - Zinc 15-30mg/day (thyroid function) [B]
  - Vitamin D (autoimmune support) [B]
  - Avoid excess iodine if Hashimoto's [D]
- **Lifestyle:** Gluten-free trial (potential autoimmune cross-reactivity) [C]
- **Professional:** Full thyroid panel (TSH, free T3, free T4, anti-TPO, anti-TG)
- **When to see derm:** Hair loss + systemic symptoms; rule out other alopecia types

### 2.6 PCOS → Hormonal Acne Pattern

**Diagnostic Criteria:** Rotterdam criteria (2 of 3: oligo/anovulation, hyperandrogenism, polycystic ovaries on ultrasound)

**Skin Pattern:** Jawline/chin acne (late 20s-40s), oily skin, hirsutism, acanthosis nigricans

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Standard acne regimen (see C. acnes section) + consider Winlevi
- **Internal:**
  - Inositol (myo-inositol 2g + D-chiro-inositol 50mg) for insulin sensitivity [B]
  - Spearmint tea (anti-androgen) [B]
  - Metformin 500-2000mg (insulin resistance) [A]
  - Spironolactone 50-200mg (anti-androgen) [A]
  - OCPs (regulate cycle) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Low-glycemic diet; regular exercise; weight loss (5-10% improves symptoms) [A]
- **Professional:** Endocrinology or reproductive endocrinology; metabolic monitoring
- **When to see derm:** If not responding to hormonal therapy; need for isotretinoin

### 2.7 DHEA-S → Skin Aging

**Mechanism:** DHEA-S is the most abundant circulating steroid; declines with age (adrenopause). Precursor to sex steroids in peripheral tissues.

| Effect | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Epidermal thickness maintenance | B |
| Sebum production (youthful levels) | B |
| Immune function in skin | C |
| Protection against photodamage | C |

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** DHEA 25-50mg/day (women; monitor androgenic side effects) [B]
- **Topical:** DHEA creams (limited research) [D]
- **Professional:** Baseline DHEA-S level; monitor for androgenic effects
- **When to see derm:** Before starting DHEA supplementation; monitor for acne/hirsutism

## 3. NUTRITION-SKIN PATHWAYS

### 3.1 Micronutrient Deficiencies & Skin

#### Zinc Deficiency → Acne, Wound Healing
- **Mechanism:** Cofactor for >300 enzymes; essential for immune function, collagen synthesis, DNA repair, keratinocyte proliferation
- **Signs:** Acne, delayed wound healing, hair loss, dermatitis around orifices
- **Serum level:** <70 mcg/dL (functional deficiency may exist at "normal" levels)

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** Zinc gluconate 30mg/day or zinc picolinate 15-30mg/day [A for acne]
- **Topical:** Zinc oxide (physical sunscreen + soothing) [A]
- **Food sources:** Oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds, legumes
- **Professional:** Serum zinc + copper ratio (excess zinc depletes copper)
- **Evidence grade:** A for acne treatment; A for wound healing

#### Vitamin D → Barrier Function, Immune Regulation
- **Mechanism:** Regulates cathelicidin (antimicrobial peptide), tight junction proteins, Treg differentiation
- **Deficiency linked to:** Psoriasis, atopic dermatitis, acne, vitiligo, alopecia areata
- **Optimal:** 40-80 ng/mL (higher end for autoimmune skin conditions)

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** Vitamin D3 2000-5000 IU/day (adjust to serum level) [A]
- **Topical:** Calcipotriene (psoriasis) [A]
- **Professional:** 25-OH vitamin D testing; recheck in 3 months
- **Evidence grade:** A for psoriasis; B for acne, eczema, alopecia areata

#### Omega-3 (EPA/DHA) → Anti-inflammatory, Barrier Support
- **Mechanism:** Precursors to resolvins/protectins (pro-resolving mediators); compete with omega-6 for COX/LOX; modulate inflammation
- **Benefits:** Reduce acne lesion count, improve barrier, reduce UV-induced inflammation

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** EPA+DHA 2-4g/day (higher EPA ratio for inflammation) [A]
- **Topical:** Omega-3/6 fatty acid creams (Eucerin, etc.) [B]
- **Food sources:** Fatty fish (salmon, sardines, mackerel), algae oil (vegan DHA)
- **Evidence grade:** A for anti-inflammatory; B for acne

#### Vitamin C → Collagen Synthesis
- **Mechanism:** Essential cofactor for prolyl and lysyl hydroxylase (collagen crosslinking); antioxidant
- **Benefits:** Wrinkle reduction, photoprotection, wound healing, hyperpigmentation

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** L-ascorbic acid 15-20% (pH <3.5 for penetration) [A]
- **Topical:** Ascorbyl tetraisopalmitate (stable, lipid-soluble) [B]
- **Internal:** Vitamin C 500-1000mg/day [B]
- **Food sources:** Citrus, kiwi, bell peppers, strawberries
- **Evidence grade:** A for topical; B for oral

#### Vitamin A/Retinoids → Cell Turnover
- **Mechanism:** Binds RAR/RXR → regulates gene transcription → normalized desquamation, collagen synthesis
- **Deficiency signs:** Follicular hyperkeratosis, night blindness, xerosis

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Tretinoin 0.025-0.1%, adapalene 0.1-0.3%, tazarotene 0.1% [A]
- **Internal:** Retinol 2500-5000 IU/day (maintenance); NOT high-dose for skin [D]
- **Food sources:** Liver, eggs, dairy, beta-carotene (plants)
- **Professional:** Pregnancy test before oral/topical retinoids (teratogenic)
- **Evidence grade:** A for topical retinoids; D for oral vitamin A for skin

#### B Vitamins → Barrier, Energy Metabolism

| Vitamin | Skin Role | Deficiency Signs | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| B3 (Niacinamide) | Barrier lipids, anti-inflammatory | Pellagra (dermatitis, dementia, diarrhea) | A |
| B5 (Pantothenic acid) | CoA synthesis, wound healing | Rare; acne improvement claimed | B |
| B7 (Biotin) | Keratin structure | Hair loss, dermatitis (rare) | C |
| B12 | Cell turnover | Hyperpigmentation, acneiform eruption | B |
| Folate | DNA synthesis | Megaloblastic changes in mucosa | C |

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Niacinamide 2-10% (best evidence) [A]
- **Internal:** B-complex (methylated forms if MTHFR); avoid high-dose biotin (interferes with lab tests)
- **Evidence grade:** A for niacinamide topical; B for pantothenic acid in acne

#### Selenium → Antioxidant Defense
- **Mechanism:** Cofactor for glutathione peroxidase; thyroid hormone metabolism; immune function
- **Deficiency linked to:** Poor wound healing, hypothyroidism-related skin changes
- **Caution:** Selenium excess ("selenosis") can cause hair loss, nail changes

**Interventions:**
- **Internal:** Selenium 100-200mcg/day (Brazil nuts = ~68-91mcg each) [B]
- **Topical:** Selenium sulfide (seborrheic dermatitis) [A]
- **Professional:** Serum selenium testing if supplementing long-term

#### Copper → Collagen Cross-linking
- **Mechanism:** Cofactor for lysyl oxidase (collagen/elastin crosslinking); antioxidant (SOD)
- **Caution:** Must balance with zinc (compete for absorption)

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Copper peptides (GHK-Cu) 0.5-2% [C]
- **Internal:** 1-2mg/day (if deficient; rare) [D]
- **Food sources:** Shellfish, nuts, seeds, organ meats
- **Evidence grade:** C for topical peptides; D for oral

### 3.2 Dietary Factors & Skin

#### Glycation → AGEs → Collagen Crosslinking
- **Mechanism:** Reducing sugars bind amino groups on proteins → Maillard reaction → AGEs → brown, stiff, crosslinked collagen
- **Skin effects:** Yellowing, stiffness, wrinkles, loss of elasticity ("sugar face")
- **Accelerated by:** UV exposure, smoking, high-AGE diet

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Carnosine 1%, aminoguanidine [C]
- **Internal:** Carnosine 500mg/day; benfotiamine (B1) 150-300mg/day [C]
- **Lifestyle:** Low-glycemic diet; avoid high-temperature cooking (grilling, frying); marinate in lemon/vinegar
- **Evidence grade:** C (preclinical strong; human trials limited)

#### Dairy → IGF-1 → Acne
- **Mechanism:** Milk contains bioactive IGF-1 and stimulates endogenous IGF-1 production; whey protein particularly problematic
- **Evidence:** Skim milk > whole milk; association stronger with acne severity [B]

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Elimination trial (4-6 weeks); try A2 milk or fermented dairy (yogurt less problematic)
- **Alternatives:** Oat, almond, coconut milk; pea protein instead of whey
- **Evidence grade:** B (association strong; RCTs limited)

#### Sugar → Insulin Spikes → Inflammation
- **Mechanism:** High glycemic load → insulin/IGF-1 → sebum, keratinocyte proliferation, inflammation
- **Skin effects:** Acne, accelerated aging (glycation), rosacea flares

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Low-glycemic diet (GI <55); minimize refined sugar; fiber with meals
- **Internal:** See insulin resistance section (berberine, chromium, ALA)
- **Evidence grade:** A for acne; A for glycation

#### Gluten → Zonulin → Leaky Gut
- **Mechanism:** Gluten triggers zonulin release → tight junction loosening → intestinal permeability; autoimmune cross-reactivity (molecular mimicry)
- **Relevant for:** Dermatitis herpetiformis (celiac), psoriasis, eczema (some), alopecia areata

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Strict gluten-free trial (6-12 weeks) for suspected cases
- **Professional:** Celiac panel (tTG-IgA, EMA, total IgA); dermatitis herpetiformis = celiac disease
- **Evidence grade:** A for dermatitis herpetiformis; C for other skin conditions

#### Alcohol → Dehydration, Nutrient Depletion
- **Mechanism:** Diuretic; depletes B vitamins, zinc, vitamin A; increases inflammation; impairs sleep
- **Skin effects:** Dehydration, flushing (rosacea trigger), accelerated aging, puffiness, dullness

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Limit to <7 drinks/week; hydrate between drinks; avoid before bed
- **Internal:** B-complex, NAC 600mg/day (glutathione support), milk thistle [C]
- **Topical:** Hydrating serums (HA, glycerin), antioxidants next morning
- **Evidence grade:** B (well-established mechanism; intervention trials limited)

## 4. LIFESTYLE-SKIN PATHWAYS

### 4.1 Sleep Deprivation

**Mechanism:**
- Reduced growth hormone secretion (peaks in deep sleep; essential for tissue repair)
- Elevated cortisol (breaks down collagen)
- Impaired barrier recovery (transepidermal water loss increases)
- Reduced melatonin (potent antioxidant)
- Poor lymphatic drainage (puffiness, dark circles)

**Skin Manifestations:** Dullness, dark circles, accelerated aging, acne flares, impaired healing

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** 7-9 hours; consistent schedule; cool, dark room; avoid screens 1h before bed
- **Internal:** Melatonin 0.3-3mg (if needed; antioxidant benefit at low dose) [B]
- **Internal:** Magnesium glycinate 200-400mg before bed [B]
- **Topical:** Overnight repair products (retinol, peptides, growth factors)
- **Evidence grade:** B (mechanism well-established; skin-specific RCTs limited)

### 4.2 Chronic Stress

**Mechanism:** See cortisol section (2.1); additionally: neuropeptide release (substance P) in skin → mast cell activation, inflammation

**Skin Manifestations:** Flares of acne, eczema, psoriasis, rosacea; delayed wound healing; telogen effluvium

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) 8-week program [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Yoga, tai chi, nature exposure ("forest bathing") [B]
- **Lifestyle:** Social connection, therapy if needed
- **Internal:** Adaptogens (ashwagandha, rhodiola, holy basil) [B]
- **Topical:** Barrier support during stress flares
- **Evidence grade:** A for stress-skin connection; B for interventions

### 4.3 Exercise

**Mechanism:** Improved circulation → nutrient delivery; sweating → toxin elimination; reduced cortisol; improved insulin sensitivity; enhanced lymphatic flow

**Skin Benefits:** Glow from circulation; stress reduction; improved sleep; detox support

**Cautions:** Sweat can trigger acne/rosacea if left on skin; friction → acne mechanica

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** 150min moderate exercise/week; cleanse face within 30 min post-workout
- **Topical:** Non-comedogenic sunscreen for outdoor exercise; barrier cream for swimmers
- **Evidence grade:** B (associational; mechanistic rationale strong)

### 4.4 Smoking

**Mechanism:** Vasoconstriction → reduced blood flow; MMP activation → collagen breakdown; oxidative stress; DNA damage; glycation acceleration

**Skin Effects:** Premature aging ("smoker's face"), grayish complexion, perioral wrinkles, delayed wound healing, psoriasis risk increased

**Interventions:**
- **Lifestyle:** Smoking cessation (biggest single anti-aging intervention) [A]
- **Topical:** Antioxidants (vitamin C, E, ferulic acid); retinoids [A]
- **Internal:** NAC 600mg 2x/day (glutathione precursor; helps quit) [B]
- **Evidence grade:** A for damage; A for cessation benefit

### 4.5 UV Exposure

**Mechanism:** UVA (deep penetration, oxidative damage, immunosuppression); UVB (DNA damage, burns, vitamin D synthesis); both → photoaging, photocarcinogenesis

**Skin Effects:** Wrinkles, sagging, hyperpigmentation, actinic keratosis, skin cancer

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Broad-spectrum SPF 30-50 daily (reapply every 2h outdoors) [A]
- **Topical:** Antioxidants morning (vitamin C, E, ferulic, resveratrol) [B]
- **Topical:** Retinoids evening (repair) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Shade-seeking; UPF clothing; avoid peak hours (10am-4pm)
- **Internal:** Heliocare/Polypodium leucotomos 240mg (oral photoprotection) [B]
- **Professional:** Annual skin checks; treat AKs before progression
- **Evidence grade:** A for sunscreen; A for retinoids

### 4.6 Blue Light (HEV)

**Mechanism:** 400-500nm light penetrates deeper than UV; generates ROS; may worsen hyperpigmentation (especially in darker skin types); disrupts circadian rhythm

**Skin Effects:** Oxidative stress, potential pigmentation, circadian disruption

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Antioxidants (iron chelators particularly; blue light generates ROS via iron) [C]
- **Lifestyle:** Screen filters; night mode; limit exposure before bed
- **Topical:** Some sunscreens now include HEV filters [C]
- **Evidence grade:** C (mechanistic data strong; long-term human data limited)

### 4.7 Pollution (Particulate Matter)

**Mechanism:** PM2.5 and PAHs penetrate skin barrier → ROS generation, DNA damage, inflammation, barrier disruption; activation of AHR (aryl hydrocarbon receptor)

**Skin Effects:** Accelerated aging, hyperpigmentation, inflammatory skin conditions, sensitive skin

**Interventions:**
- **Topical:** Double cleanse (oil + water-based) to remove particulates [B]
- **Topical:** Antioxidants (vitamin C, E, niacinamide, resveratrol) [B]
- **Topical:** Barrier repair (ceramides) [A]
- **Internal:** Omega-3 (anti-inflammatory) [A]
- **Lifestyle:** Air purifier (HEPA) at home; avoid outdoor exercise during high pollution
- **Evidence grade:** B (epidemiological strong; intervention trials emerging)

### 5.2 Evidence Grade Key

| Grade | Definition | Reliability |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Strong evidence from RCTs, meta-analyses, or systematic reviews | High confidence |
| **B** | Moderate evidence from smaller RCTs, cohort studies, or consistent observational data | Moderate confidence |
| **C** | Limited evidence from small trials, case series, or strong mechanistic rationale | Lower confidence; may work |
| **D** | Expert consensus, preclinical/animal data, or theoretical mechanism | Use with caution |

### 5.3 When to See a Dermatologist — General Guidelines

| Scenario | Urgency | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Changing/evolving mole | **Urgent** | Rule out melanoma |
| Sudden widespread rash | **Urgent** | Drug reaction, viral exanthem, autoimmune |
| Severe acne with nodules/cysts | High | Prevent scarring; need prescription |
| Suspected psoriasis new diagnosis | High | Confirm; assess for arthritis |
| Hair loss patches | Moderate | Alopecia areata, tinea, scarring alopecia |
| Persistent eczema not responding | Moderate | Consider allergy testing, biologics |
| Rosacea with eye involvement | Moderate | Ocular rosacea needs treatment |
| Hyperpigmentation widespread | Low-Moderate | Melasma vs other; treatment plan |
| Routine anti-aging | Low | Prescription retinoids, procedures |
| Cosmetic concerns | Low | Elective; set expectations |

### 6.3 The Gut-Skin Protocol (Summary)

For skin conditions with suspected gut involvement (acne, rosacea, eczema, psoriasis):

1. **Remove:** Eliminate triggers (dairy, gluten trial if indicated; reduce sugar)
2. **Replace:** Digestive support (HCl, enzymes if low stomach acid)
3. **Reinoculate:** Probiotics (10^9-10^10 CFU multi-strain) + prebiotics (5-10g fiber)
4. **Repair:** L-glutamine 5g/day; zinc carnosine 75mg 2x/day; bone broth/collagen
5. **Rebalance:** Stress management; sleep; anti-inflammatory diet (omega-3 rich)
6. **Timeframe:** 8-12 weeks minimum before reassessment

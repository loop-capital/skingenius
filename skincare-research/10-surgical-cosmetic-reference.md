# Surgical Cosmetic Procedures: Reference Guide

> **SKINgenius Clinical Reference | Last Updated: May 2026**
>
> **IMPORTANT:** This document is for EDUCATION AND REFERENCE ONLY. SKINgenius does NOT recommend surgical procedures. The purpose is to help users understand what surgery addresses, when non-surgical options are insufficient, and when to refer to a board-certified plastic surgeon. Always redirect surgical questions to qualified medical professionals.

## Overview
Surgical cosmetic procedures address concerns that skincare, injectables, and energy-based devices cannot adequately correct — primarily significant skin laxity, structural changes, and volume redistribution from aging or weight loss. SKINgenius includes this reference so we can have honest conversations about limitations of non-surgical options and guide appropriate referrals.

## When SKINgenius Should Refer to a Surgeon (Decision Framework)

| Sign | What It Means | Recommended Action |
|------|--------------|-------------------|
| >2cm skin pinch on jawline | Significant laxity beyond non-surgical correction | Refer to board-certified plastic surgeon |
| Deep nasolabial folds at rest | Structural volume shift, not just line-deepening | Consider fillers first; refer if insufficient |
| Turkey neck / platysmal bands | Muscle banding requires surgical platysmaplasty | Refer — no non-surgical equivalent |
| Hooded eyes obstructing vision | Functional + cosmetic concern | Refer to oculoplastic or plastic surgeon |
| Significant post-weight-loss skin excess | Skin elasticity gone; no device will tighten enough | Refer for excisional surgery |
| Patient wants dramatic, long-lasting change | Non-surgical will disappoint | Set expectations or refer |
| Repeated non-surgical treatments not delivering | Diminishing returns indicate structural problem | Honest referral |

**Board Certification Matters:** Always recommend **American Board of Plastic Surgery** (ABPS) certified surgeons. "Cosmetic surgeon" is NOT the same — any physician can claim this title regardless of training.

## FACIAL PROCEDURES

### 1. Facelift (Rhytidectomy)

**What it is:** Surgical redraping and tightening of facial skin and underlying SMAS (superficial musculoaponeurotic system) layer to address mid-to-lower face aging, jowling, and neck laxity.

**Variants:**
| Type | Technique | Best For | Recovery | Longevity |
|------|-----------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Deep Plane** | Releases SMAS-lifts as single unit | Moderate-severe laxity | 3–4 weeks | 10–15 years |
| **SMAS Facelift** | Elevates SMAS separately from skin | Moderate laxity | 2–3 weeks | 8–12 years |
| **Mini/Short Scar** | Limited incision, less dissection | Mild laxity, younger patients | 1–2 weeks | 5–7 years |

**Cost:** $10,000–$25,000 (varies by region and surgeon)
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — gold standard for facial aging; well-studied with decades of outcomes data
**Key Risk:** Nerve injury (marginal mandibular branch ~1–3%), hematoma, scarring, asymmetry
**What SKINgenius can't achieve:** Actual tissue repositioning and excess skin removal. Fillers can simulate volume but cannot replicate surgical lifting.

---

### 2. Blepharoplasty (Eyelid Surgery)

**What it is:** Surgical removal of excess skin and fat from upper and/or lower eyelids.

**Variants:**
- **Upper blepharoplasty:** Removes hooded skin; may be covered by insurance if obstructing vision
- **Lower blepharoplasty:** Addresses under-eye bags; may include fat repositioning
- **Transconjunctival:** No external incision; for fat removal only (lower lids)

**Cost:** $3,000–$8,000 (upper); $4,000–$10,000 (lower); $6,000–$15,000 (both)
**Recovery:** 1–2 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — well-established procedure with high satisfaction
**What SKINgenius can't achieve:** Excess skin removal. Fillers can camouflage hollows but cannot address hooding or fat pads.

---

### 3. Rhinoplasty (Nose Surgery)

**What it is:** Surgical reshaping of the nose for aesthetic and/or functional reasons.

**Variants:**
- **Open rhinoplasty:** External incision — better access, more dramatic changes
- **Closed rhinoplasty:** Internal incisions only — less visible scarring, limited access
- **Liquid rhinoplasty (non-surgical):** Filler-based — temporary, carries vascular occlusion risk in nose

**Cost:** $5,000–$15,000 (surgical); $600–$1,500 (liquid — temporary)
**Recovery:** 1–2 weeks initial; 6–12 months for final result
**Evidence Tier:** Strong for surgical; Weak for liquid (no permanent correction, rare but serious blindness risk)
**Key Note:** Liquid rhinoplasty is high-risk — the nose is a danger zone for vascular occlusion. SKINgenius should flag this clearly.

---

### 4. Brow Lift (Forehead Lift)

**What it is:** Surgical elevation of the brow and forehead to address hooding, deep forehead lines, and brow ptosis.

**Variants:** Endoscopic (minimal scars), coronal (classic), direct (for lateral brow only)
**Cost:** $4,000–$10,000
**Recovery:** 2–3 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong
**What SKINgenius can't achieve:** Botox can lift the brow 1–3mm; surgery lifts 5–15mm.

---

### 5. Neck Lift (Cervicoplasty / Platysmaplasty)

**What it is:** Surgical correction of neck aging — excess skin, fat, and platysmal banding.

**Variants:**
- **Platysmaplasty:** Tightens the neck muscle bands
- **Cervicoplasty:** Removes excess neck skin
- **Submental lipectomy:** Removes neck fat (often combined)

**Cost:** $5,000–$12,000
**Recovery:** 2–3 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong
**What SKINgenius can't achieve:** No topical or injectable can address platysmal bands or significant neck skin excess. This is the procedure where non-surgical options are most limited.

---

### 6. Otoplasty (Ear Surgery)

**What it is:** Surgical correction of prominent or misshapen ears.
**Cost:** $3,000–$6,000
**Recovery:** 1–2 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — well-established, high satisfaction

---

### 7. Buccal Fat Removal

**What it is:** Surgical removal of buccal fat pads to create cheek hollows and facial definition.

**⚠️ Controversial:** Trendy but increasingly criticized. Removing buccal fat accelerates mid-face aging — patients often regret it in their 40s–50s as natural fat loss continues.
**Cost:** $2,000–$5,000
**Recovery:** 1–2 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Moderate — procedure is established, but long-term aging consequences are under-discussed
**SKINgenius Note:** Should flag the aging concern honestly. Not reversible.

## BODY PROCEDURES

### 8. Breast Augmentation

**What it is:** Surgical breast enhancement via implants (saline or silicone) or fat transfer.
**Cost:** $5,000–$12,000 (implants); $6,000–$15,000 (fat transfer)
**Recovery:** 4–6 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — most studied cosmetic surgical procedure
**Key Risks:** Capsular contracture (10–15%), implant rupture, BIA-ALCL (rare but serious lymphoma), need for revision surgery within 10–15 years
**2026 Trend:** Fat transfer breast augmentation growing; silicone implant safety concerns ongoing

---

### 9. Breast Lift (Mastopexy)

**What it is:** Surgical lifting and reshaping of sagging breasts without significant volume change.
**Cost:** $5,000–$12,000
**Recovery:** 4–6 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong

---

### 10. Breast Reduction

**What it is:** Surgical removal of excess breast tissue and skin for functional and aesthetic improvement.
**Cost:** $5,000–$15,000 (often partially covered by insurance)
**Recovery:** 4–6 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — high functional satisfaction rates

---

### 11. Liposuction

**What it is:** Surgical fat removal via suction cannula.

**Variants:**
| Type | Technique | Best For | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Tumescent** | Fluid injection + suction | Large volume fat removal | $3,000–$8,000/area |
| **Power-Assisted (PAL)** | Vibrating cannula | Fibrous areas, larger volumes | $3,500–$10,000 |
| **Laser-Assisted (SmartLipo)** | Laser melts fat before removal | Smaller areas, skin tightening | $3,000–$8,000 |
| **Ultrasound-Assisted (VASER)** | Ultrasound emulsifies fat | Precise contouring | $4,000–$10,000 |

**Evidence Tier:** Strong — gold standard for fat removal
**Key Point:** Liposuction is NOT a weight loss tool. It's for contouring. Best for BMI <30 with localized deposits.

---

### 12. Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty)

**What it is:** Surgical removal of excess abdominal skin and fat, often with muscle repair (diastasis recti).

**Variants:** Full, mini, extended (includes flanks)
**Cost:** $6,000–$20,000
**Recovery:** 4–8 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong — especially post-pregnancy or massive weight loss
**What SKINgenius can't achieve:** No topical or device can remove excess skin or repair separated abdominal muscles.

---

### 13. BBL (Brazilian Butt Lift)

**What it is:** Fat transfer to the buttocks — liposuction from one area, purification, reinjection into glutes.

**⚠️ CRITICAL SAFETY CONCERN:** BBL has the highest mortality rate of any cosmetic procedure (~1 in 3,000–5,000). Death occurs when fat is injected into or near deep veins, causing pulmonary fat embolism. The Aesthetic Society issued specific safety guidelines in 2018 requiring subcutaneous-only injection.

**Cost:** $8,000–$20,000
**Recovery:** 6–8 weeks (cannot sit on buttocks for 2–4 weeks)
**Evidence Tier:** Moderate — high satisfaction when performed safely, but significant safety variability between providers
**SKINgenius Note:** If a user asks about BBL, flag the mortality risk clearly and recommend ABPS-certified surgeons only.

---

### 14. Mommy Makeover

**What it is:** Combined surgical package typically including breast augmentation/lift, tummy tuck, and liposuction.
**Cost:** $15,000–$35,000
**Recovery:** 6–8 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Moderate — each procedure individually has strong evidence; combined procedures increase risk
**Key Risk:** Extended anesthesia time increases complication risk. Staged procedures may be safer.

---

### 15. Arm Lift (Brachioplasty)

**What it is:** Surgical removal of excess upper arm skin and fat.
**Cost:** $4,000–$10,000
**Recovery:** 3–4 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Strong for significant excess skin
**Key Issue:** Visible scarring along inner arm. Non-surgical options (Thermage, EMSCULPT) minimal for significant laxity.

---

### 16. Thigh Lift

**What it is:** Surgical removal of excess thigh skin (inner and/or outer).
**Cost:** $5,000–$12,000
**Recovery:** 4–6 weeks
**Evidence Tier:** Moderate — significant scarring tradeoff

## EMERGING CONCERNS

### 17. "Ozempic Face/Body" Phenomenon

GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide/Ozempic, tirzepatide/Mounjaro) are driving unprecedented demand for cosmetic procedures. Rapid fat loss causes:
- **"Ozempic face"**: Volume loss in cheeks, temples, and periorbital area
- **"Ozempic body"**: Skin laxity from rapid weight loss
- **Surgical demand surge**: 40%+ increase in facelifts, tummy tucks, and body lifts attributed to GLP-1 users (ASPS 2024 data)

**SKINgenius relevance:** Users on GLP-1 medications will increasingly ask about fillers (for volume), skin tightening (for laxity), and eventually surgery (for excess skin). Honest guidance about staged approaches is critical — don't fill aggressively during active weight loss.

### 18. Gender-Affirming Procedures

Brief reference for inclusivity: Facial feminization surgery (FFS), chest procedures (top surgery), body contouring, and genital surgeries are performed by specialized surgeons. These are typically insurance-variable and require multidisciplinary care. SKINgenius should provide respectful, knowledgeable referral guidance without gatekeeping.

## SKINgenius Position on Surgical Referrals

1. **Be honest about non-surgical limitations.** If someone has significant laxity, don't oversell tightening devices — they'll spend $10K+ and still be disappointed.

2. **Board certification is non-negotiable.** Recommend only ABPS-certified surgeons. "Board-certified cosmetic surgeon" is NOT the same qualification.

3. **Timing matters.** GLP-1 patients should stabilize weight before fillers or surgery. Active weight loss = poor candidate for volume restoration.

4. **Staged approaches work best.** Start with skincare + injectables → try energy-based → refer for surgery when non-surgical options plateau.

5. **Cost transparency.** Non-surgical treatments can approach surgical costs over time. Be honest about the math: $3,000/year in fillers for 5 years = surgical facelift that lasts 10+ years.

6. **Never minimize surgical risk.** BBL mortality, capsular contracture, anesthesia complications — these are real. Users deserve informed consent-level honesty.

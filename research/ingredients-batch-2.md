## Ingredient 014: Collagen / Hydrolyzed Collagen

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **INCI Name** | Hydrolyzed Collagen |
| **Common Names** | Collagen Peptides, Marine Collagen, Bovine Collagen, Hydrolyzed Collagen Protein |
| **Category** | Protein |
| **Function** | Provides amino acids for skin structure; film-forming hydration; stimulates endogenous collagen production when delivered effectively |

### Skin Benefits
- **Hydration:** Excellent humectant; hydrolyzed forms penetrate better than native collagen
- **Anti-aging:** Film-forming properties plump fine lines temporarily
- **Barrier support:** Amino acid delivery supports barrier repair

### Conditions Treated
| Condition | Evidence | Grade | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|-------|
| Fine lines/wrinkles | Moderate | C+ | Cannot directly replace dermal collagen due to molecular size; cosmetic benefit through surface hydration |
| Dehydration | Moderate | B | Excellent humectant; hydrolyzed forms penetrate better |
| Loss of firmness | Limited | C | Primarily cosmetic benefit through plumping |

### Mechanism of Action
1. **Film-forming:** Creates moisture-retentive barrier on skin surface
2. **Amino acid delivery:** Provides glycine, proline, hydroxyproline for skin's collagen synthesis
3. **Humectant:** Binds water to improve skin hydration
4. **Signaling:** Some peptides may signal fibroblasts to increase collagen production

### Safety Profile
| Factor | Status |
|----------|--------|
| **Pregnancy** | Safe |
| **Breastfeeding** | Safe |
| **Allergies** | Rare; fish/shellfish allergies check source |
| **Sensitivities** | Very well tolerated |
| **Interactions** | None known |

**Cautions:**
- Cannot replace injectable collagen or stimulate significant dermal collagen production alone
- Marine source may trigger fish allergies

### Concentration & pH
- **Concentration:** 1-5% (cosmetic); oral 2.5-10g/day shows better evidence
- **Optimal pH:** 5.0–7.0

### Root Cause Addressed
Amino acid deficiency in skin matrix; surface dehydration

### Related Ingredients
Peptides, Hyaluronic Acid, Ceramides, Amino Acids

## Ingredient 017: Tranexamic Acid

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **INCI Name** | Tranexamic Acid |
| **Common Names** | TXA, Transamin, Melasma Treatment Acid |
| **Category** | Brightening Agent |
| **Function** | Inhibits melanin production; reduces hyperpigmentation, melasma, PIH |

### Skin Benefits
- **Hyperpigmentation:** Inhibits UV-induced melanin synthesis
- **Melasma:** Oral form is gold-standard; topical shows significant improvement
- **Anti-inflammatory:** Decreases inflammatory mediators triggering melanin
- **Vascular:** Reduces blood vessel growth associated with melasma

### Conditions Treated
| Condition | Evidence | Grade | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|-------|
| Melasma | Strong | A- | Oral TXA is gold-standard; topical effective with fewer risks |
| PIH | Strong | B+ | Effective, especially in darker skin tones |
| Hyperpigmentation | Strong | A- | Multiple RCTs support efficacy |
| Rosacea | Limited | C+ | Anti-inflammatory properties may help |

### Mechanism of Action
1. **Plasmin inhibition:** Blocks plasminogen activator
2. **Melanin suppression:** Reduces UV-induced melanin synthesis
3. **Anti-inflammatory:** Decreases inflammatory mediators
4. **Vascular:** Reduces blood vessel growth in melasma

### Safety Profile
| Factor | Status |
|----------|--------|
| **Pregnancy** | Avoid oral; topical likely safe but limited data |
| **Breastfeeding** | Avoid oral; topical safety not established |
| **Allergies** | Rare |
| **Sensitivities** | Generally well tolerated |

**Cautions:**
- Oral form requires medical supervision (thromboembolic risk)
- Topical form much safer with minimal systemic absorption
- Do not combine oral with hormonal contraceptives or anticoagulants without supervision

### Concentration & pH
- **Concentration:** Topical 2-5%; Oral 250-500mg 2x daily (prescription)
- **Optimal pH:** 7.0–8.0 (works at neutral pH)

### Root Cause Addressed
UV-induced melanin overproduction; inflammatory melanin triggers; vascular melasma component

### Related Ingredients
Vitamin C, Niacinamide, Kojic Acid, Azelaic Acid, Hydroquinone

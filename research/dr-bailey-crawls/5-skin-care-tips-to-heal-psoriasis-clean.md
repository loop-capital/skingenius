## Dermatologist's tips for the best skin care to help heal psoriasis.
When I see a patient with psoriasis, I always start our discussion about treatment options with detailed information on hydrating skin care, because skin care really is that important for psoriasis.
If you have psoriasis you need to know that the rash of psoriasis gets worse when your skin is dry, injured, and unhealthy. It is called the Koebner Phenomenon. It means that maintaining well-moisturized skin is the single most important step for your psoriasis skin care.

## Dermatologist's 5 Skin Care Tips to Help Heal Psoriasis
My 6 key goals for skin care that heals and prevents psoriasis are:
  1. "Lock and load" water into the skin 24/7.
  2. Use skin care products that contain ingredients that slow skin cell turnover. The best example of this are the products that contain tar.
  3. Use skin care products that use keratolytics to exfoliate excessive scale. This is like a 'chemical peel' for psoriasis done gently and daily. Examples include BHAs (salicylic acid) and AHAs (lactic and glycolic acids).
  4. Address facial seborrhea (a.k.a. dandruff) because it often coexists with psoriasis in the crossover variant called sebo-psoriasis.
  5. Treat scalp psoriasis with shampoos and leave-on treatments.
  6. Look for and treat foot fungus, because it's extremely common and can look like psoriasis, many psoriasis patients have it on their feet, and it's just one more rash that psoriasis patients don't need to suffer from.

Here are the details...
## 1. Deeply hydrate skin to heal psoriasis. 
Holding water in your skin is what moisturizing is all about. You have to do it from the outside; drinking lots of water won't moisturize your skin any more than taking a bath will quench your thirst.
#### You need to know the two important rules for skin hydration.
  * First, don't dry out your skin by overusing soap.
  * Second, do apply an effective moisturizer to all your skin within 3 minutes of toweling off after every bath or shower.

The reason optimal skin hydration is so important to your psoriasis is because of the Koebner Phenomenon. This phenomenon is the observation that the rash of psoriasis tends to flare up on injured skin. Because dry skin is injured skin, you need to prevent skin dryness. Thus, you need to:
### To heal psoriasis, you need to avoid drying out your skin with soap.
Limit soap exposure to only those areas that need it. On the average day, those are:
  * The body odor areas including parts of your skin with body odor glands called apocrine glands. These sites are the armpits, groin, and buttocks. Feet smell too so soap them up.
  * The oily areas of your skin which are the face, ears, back, and chest.

## 3. AHA (glycolic acid) and BHA (salicylic acid) skin care to heal psoriasis.
AHAs and BHAs are keratolytics meaning they help reduce the scale on psoriasis lesions. Keratolytics act like chemical peels in that they loosen the connections between dead skin scale cells so that the cells can shed more quickly. AHAs also helps bind water to the skin for extra skin hydration. AHAs include lactic acid and glycolic acid.

## 5. Scalp skin care to heal psoriasis
Scalp psoriasis can look like a really stubborn case of scalp dandruff. The scale is often silver and very thick. I always start with a rotation of tar and salicylic acid shampoos. Lather the scalp with the medicated shampoo, leave it on for 5 minutes then rinse using the tar shampoo one day and the salicylic acid shampoo the next. If that does not work, we pretreat with oil alternating with P&S Liquid which contains salicylic acid. I describe scalp treatment in this article.
I am often asked about glycolic acid for scalp psoriasis. There are no products specifically designed for scalp psoriasis that contain glycolic acid. That said, scalp skin is skin and you can use skin care products with glycolic acid on the scalp such as the **Glycolic Acid Professional Body Lotion** above. 
## 6. Foot care to heal psoriasis. 
Athlete's foot is so common and the scaling, fissured, and itchy rash looks so similar to psoriasis. Plus, thick psoriatic scale is a bounty for the fungus that causes athlete's foot. I've found that many psoriasis patients have a positive culture test for fungus on their feet and even their nails. Foot psoriasis can be painful and fungus can make symptoms worse.

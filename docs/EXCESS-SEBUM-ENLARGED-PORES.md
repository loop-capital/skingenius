# Excess Sebum & Enlarged Pores — Condition Knowledge Base

> Reference for the three-tier recommendation model

## Biological Foundation
Pore size is primarily driven by two things: clogged sebum stretching the follicle walls, and the loss of collagen and skin elasticity around the pore opening as we age.

You cannot technically change the genetic "blueprint" size of your pores, but you can absolutely change how stretched out they are.

By addressing both the internal drivers of sebum production and the structural integrity of the skin, you can dramatically tighten and refine skin texture.

## Tier 1: Topical Regulation (The Daily Routine)

### Salicylic Acid (BHA) at 2%
Unlike water-soluble Alpha-Hydroxy Acids (like glycolic acid), BHAs are oil-soluble. This allows them to penetrate deep inside the lipid-rich pore matrix to dissolve the sticky mix of sebum and dead skin cells before it stretches the pore.

### Niacinamide (3% to 5%)
A clinical workhorse for sebum regulation. Niacinamide structurally reduces the sebaceous lipogenesis (fat production) rate while simultaneously strengthening the skin barrier and improving elasticity around the pore walls.

### Retinoids (Retinol, Retinal, or Tretinoin)
Retinoids normalize skin cell turnover (preventing cells from shedding inside the pore and causing plugs) and potently stimulate collagen synthesis. Rebuilding the collagen matrix "squeezes" the pore opening from the outside, making it look visibly smaller.

## Tier 2: Clinical Treatments (The Heavy Hitters)

### RF Microneedling (Radiofrequency)
Combines mechanical micro-injuries with heat energy delivered directly into the dermis. It triggers massive collagen remodeling to tighten the skin architecture around the pores and can actually thermally down-regulate overactive sebaceous glands.

### Laser Resurfacing (Non-Ablative Fraxel or Clear + Brilliant)
These lasers create micro-zones of thermal damage that force the skin to replace stretched-out, damaged tissue with fresh, highly elastic collagen.

### Micro-Tox (Intradermal Botox)
Instead of injecting Botulinum toxin into the muscle to stop wrinkles, "micro-tox" involves injecting highly diluted micro-droplets superficially into the skin layer. This temporarily paralyzes the tiny arrector pili muscles and the sebaceous glands themselves, drastically shutting down oil production and instantly making pores look airbrushed for 2 to 3 months.

## Tier 3: Systemic & Dietary Drivers (Internal Regulation)

### Hormonal Pathway
[High Glycemic Diet / Dairy] → ↑ Insulin & IGF-1 → ↑ Androgen Activity → Hyper-Sebum Production

### Insulin & IGF-1 Management
Spikes in insulin and Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 (IGF-1) directly upregulate sebum production. Shifting away from high-glycemic carbohydrates and reducing whey protein or heavy dairy intake can noticeably calm overactive sebaceous glands.

### Green Tea Extract (EGCG)
Epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG) is a potent topical and systemic 5-alpha-reductase inhibitor. By blocking the conversion of testosterone into the more potent DHT at the skin level, it reduces the hormonal signal that tells your pores to produce oil.

### Zinc Supplementation
Zinc (specifically Zinc Picolinate or Methionine) modulates total androgen activity and acts as a natural anti-inflammatory, helping to reduce the lipid content of sebum.

## App Branching Logic
**Key question for users:** "Are you currently dealing with active acne breakouts alongside the excess oil, or is your primary focus strictly on refining skin texture and reducing shine?"
- If acne branch → route to acne-vulgaris or hormonal-acne condition tree
- If texture/shine branch → stay in excess-sebum-enlarged-pores condition tree (no acne medications needed)

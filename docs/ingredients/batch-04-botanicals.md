# SKINgenius Ingredient Profiles — Batch 4: Botanicals & Natural Extracts

> **Research Date:** 2026-05-14  
> **Analyst:** SKINgenius-CEO  
> **Scope:** 20 botanical/natural ingredients commonly found in skincare formulations  
> **Methodology:** Evidence-based review of primary mechanisms, clinical data, and formulation parameters.

## Ingredient Profile: Aloe Vera
- **INCI Name:** Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Juice / Aloe Barbadensis Leaf Extract
- **Category:** Humectant, soothing agent, wound-healing promoter
- **Primary Mechanism:** Polysaccharides (acemannan) form a protective gel barrier on skin; anthraquinones and salicylic acid derivatives provide mild anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Stimulates fibroblast activity and collagen synthesis.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (well-documented traditional use; modern clinical data supports wound healing and anti-inflammatory properties)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–10% (pure gel); up to 100% in gels; 0.5–5% in emulsions
- **Cautions:** Generally recognized as safe (GRAS); rare contact dermatitis reported. Some individuals sensitive to anthraquinones. Ensure proper preservation — aloe is prone to microbial contamination.
- **Related Ingredients:** Centella Asiatica (Gotu Kola), Allantoin, Hyaluronic Acid, Chamomile

## Ingredient Profile: Apple Cider Vinegar
- **INCI Name:** Acetum / Apple Cider Vinegar (not a standardized cosmetic INCI; often listed as pH adjuster or astringent)
- **Category:** Astringent, pH adjuster, keratolytic (mild)
- **Primary Mechanism:** Acetic acid (4–6%) lowers skin pH, promoting acid mantle restoration. Contains alpha-hydroxy acids (malic acid, citric acid) that provide mild exfoliation. Antimicrobial against *Malassezia* and *Cutibacterium acnes*.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate for skincare (strong traditional use; limited controlled clinical trials in cosmetic concentrations)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% diluted in toners or rinses; rarely >10% in leave-on products due to irritation risk
- **Cautions:** **High risk of chemical burns and irritation if used undiluted.** Not recommended for sensitive or compromised skin. Avoid use with retinoids or strong acids. Pregnancy-safe in dilute cosmetic use. Can disrupt skin barrier if overused.
- **Related Ingredients:** Glycolic Acid, Lactic Acid, Salicylic Acid, Witch Hazel

## Ingredient Profile: Avocado Extract
- **INCI Name:** Persea Gratissima (Avocado) Fruit Extract / Persea Gratissima Oil
- **Category:** Emollient, occlusive, nutrient-dense oil
- **Primary Mechanism:** Rich in oleic acid (omega-9), linoleic acid, and palmitoleic acid. High content of vitamins E, C, and carotenoids provides antioxidant protection. Phytosterols (beta-sitosterol) support barrier repair and reduce inflammation. Fatty acid profile closely resembles skin's natural sebum.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (well-established emollient; some clinical data for barrier repair in atopic dermatitis when used as carrier oil)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); 1–10% (oil in emulsions); up to 100% as a facial oil
- **Cautions:** Generally safe; rare latex-fruit syndrome cross-reactivity (avocado allergy). Comedogenic rating: 2 (moderate). Safe in pregnancy.
- **Related Ingredients:** Jojoba Oil, Squalane, Shea Butter, Olive Oil

## Ingredient Profile: Bamboo Extract
- **INCI Name:** Bambusa Vulgaris Extract / Phyllostachys Edulis Extract
- **Category:** Silica source, antioxidant, skin-conditioning agent
- **Primary Mechanism:** Rich in silica (silicon dioxide), which is essential for collagen synthesis and connective tissue integrity. Contains flavonoids and phenolic acids with antioxidant activity. Provides mild mechanical exfoliation in powder form.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate for topical skincare (silica's role in skin health is more nutritional; limited direct clinical evidence for topical bamboo extract)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–3% (extract); variable in exfoliating powders
- **Cautions:** Generally safe. Silica powders can be respiratory irritants if inhaled (powder form concerns). Safe in pregnancy.
- **Related Ingredients:** Horsetail Extract (also silica-rich), Rice Bran Extract, Diatomaceous Earth

## Ingredient Profile: Calendula Extract
- **INCI Name:** Calendula Officinalis Flower Extract / Calendula Officinalis Flower Oil
- **Category:** Anti-inflammatory, wound-healing, antimicrobial
- **Primary Mechanism:** Triterpenoids (faradiol, amyrin) and flavonoids inhibit prostaglandin synthesis and reduce inflammation. Saponins and carotenoids promote granulation tissue formation and epithelialization. Demonstrated antibacterial and antifungal activity against common skin pathogens.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (supported by traditional medicine and some clinical trials for wound healing and radiation dermatitis)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); 2–10% (infused oil)
- **Cautions:** **Avoid in pregnancy** — insufficient safety data; some traditional sources contraindicate. Allergic reactions possible in individuals sensitive to Asteraceae/Compositae family (ragweed, daisies, marigolds). Patch test recommended.
- **Related Ingredients:** Chamomile, Arnica, Gotu Kola, Plantain Extract

## Ingredient Profile: Carrot Seed Oil
- **INCI Name:** Daucus Carota Sativa (Carrot) Seed Oil
- **Category:** Antioxidant, skin-conditioning, cell-regenerative
- **Primary Mechanism:** High in carotenoids (beta-carotene, lycopene) that convert to vitamin A and provide photoprotection. Rich in vitamin E (tocopherol) and vitamin C. Sesquiterpenes (carotol, daucol) have antimicrobial and antifungal properties. Stimulates epidermal cell turnover.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (strong traditional/aromatherapy use; limited peer-reviewed clinical trials for cosmetic applications)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.1–1% (essential oil, always diluted); 1–5% (carrier oil blends)
- **Cautions:** **Photosensitizing risk** — beta-carotene can increase sun sensitivity in some individuals. Always use with SPF during daytime. Avoid in pregnancy (concentrated essential oil). Can stain skin orange at high concentrations. Do not confuse with carrot *root* oil (different composition).
- **Related Ingredients:** Rosehip Oil, Sea Buckthorn Oil, Retinol, Bakuchiol

## Ingredient Profile: Chamomile Extract
- **INCI Name:** Chamomilla Recutita (Matricaria) Flower Extract / Anthemis Nobilis (Roman Chamomile) Flower Extract
- **Category:** Anti-inflammatory, soothing, antimicrobial
- **Primary Mechanism:** Bisabolol and chamazulene (in German/Matricaria chamomile) inhibit leukotriene and prostaglandin synthesis, reducing inflammation. Apigenin and luteolin flavonoids provide antioxidant activity and mild anxiolytic effects (relevant for stress-related skin conditions). Alpha-bisabolol has demonstrated antimicrobial activity against *Staphylococcus aureus*.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (bisabolol is well-studied; chamomile extract has clinical support for atopic dermatitis and wound healing)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); 0.1–1% bisabolol (isolated compound)
- **Cautions:** Allergic reactions in individuals sensitive to Asteraceae/Compositae family. Rare cases of contact dermatitis. Generally considered safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations.
- **Related Ingredients:** Calendula, Azulene, Allantoin, Green Tea Extract

## Ingredient Profile: Coconut Oil
- **INCI Name:** Cocos Nucifera (Coconut) Oil
- **Category:** Occlusive, emollient, antimicrobial
- **Primary Mechanism:** 47% lauric acid provides significant antimicrobial activity against *Staphylococcus aureus*, *Candida albicans*, and *Malassezia* species. Medium-chain fatty acids penetrate stratum corneum, supporting barrier repair. Triglycerides form occlusive film reducing transepidermal water loss (TEWL).
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate to Strong for barrier repair and antimicrobial properties (well-documented in atopic dermatitis and xerosis studies)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–10% in formulations; up to 100% as body/facial oil
- **Cautions:** **Highly comedogenic (rating: 4)** — not recommended for acne-prone or oily skin. Can exacerbate fungal acne (*Malassezia* folliculitis) despite antimicrobial properties — paradoxical effect in some individuals. Safe in pregnancy. Use fractionated coconut oil (MCT oil) for non-comedogenic alternative.
- **Related Ingredients:** MCT Oil, Babassu Oil, Palm Kernel Oil, Shea Butter

## Ingredient Profile: Cucumber Extract
- **INCI Name:** Cucumis Sativus (Cucumber) Fruit Extract
- **Category:** Soothing, astringent, hydrating
- **Primary Mechanism:** High water content (96%) provides immediate hydration. Cucurbitacins and flavonoids (quercetin, kaempferol) offer anti-inflammatory and antioxidant activity. Ascorbic acid (vitamin C) and caffeic acid reduce UV-induced oxidative stress. Natural polysaccharides form light moisturizing film.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (long history of cosmetic use; limited controlled clinical trials demonstrating efficacy beyond hydration)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); cucumber slices or juice in DIY formulations
- **Cautions:** Generally safe and well-tolerated. Rare contact dermatitis. Safe in pregnancy. Often used more for sensorial/refreshing benefits than profound therapeutic effects.
- **Related Ingredients:** Aloe Vera, Witch Hazel, Green Tea Extract, Allantoin

## Ingredient Profile: Echinacea Extract
- **INCI Name:** Echinacea Purpurea Extract / Echinacea Angustifolia Extract
- **Category:** Immunomodulatory, antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory
- **Primary Mechanism:** Echinacoside and chicoric acid modulate immune responses in skin, stimulating macrophage activity. Alkamides and polysaccharides have demonstrated antiviral and antibacterial properties. Inhibits hyaluronidase, potentially preserving extracellular matrix integrity.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate for topical skincare (strong internal use evidence; limited but promising topical data for wound healing and anti-aging)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–3% (extract)
- **Cautions:** Allergic reactions possible, especially in individuals with Asteraceae/Compositae sensitivity. Rare cases of topical irritation. **Avoid in autoimmune conditions** (theoretical immunostimulant concern, though topical risk is minimal). Safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations.
- **Related Ingredients:** Calendula, Arnica, Tea Tree Oil, Propolis

## Ingredient Profile: Frankincense Oil
- **INCI Name:** Boswellia Carterii Oil / Boswellia Serrata Extract
- **Category:** Anti-inflammatory, astringent, cell-regenerative
- **Primary Mechanism:** Boswellic acids (alpha and beta) inhibit 5-lipoxygenase, reducing leukotriene-mediated inflammation. Incensole acetate has demonstrated anti-anxiety and anti-inflammatory effects. Promotes keratinocyte differentiation and collagen synthesis in fibroblast cultures.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (boswellic acids well-studied for internal anti-inflammatory use; growing topical evidence for anti-aging and scar reduction)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.1–1% (essential oil, always diluted); 1–3% (extract)
- **Cautions:** **Avoid in pregnancy** — emmenagogue properties reported in traditional medicine (may stimulate uterine contractions). Photosensitizing potential. Skin sensitization possible with oxidized oil. Always dilute essential oil properly.
- **Related Ingredients:** Myrrh Oil, Turmeric Extract, Centella Asiatica, Resveratrol

---

## Ingredient Profile: Ginkgo Biloba Extract
- **INCI Name:** Ginkgo Biloba Leaf Extract
- **Category:** Antioxidant, vasoprotective, anti-inflammatory
- **Primary Mechanism:** Flavonoid glycosides (quercetin, kaempferol, isorhamnetin) and terpene lactones (ginkgolides, bilobalide) scavenge free radicals and reduce oxidative stress. Improves microcirculation through PAF (platelet-activating factor) antagonism. Inhibits collagenase and elastase, potentially reducing matrix degradation.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate for topical use (strong systemic evidence for cerebral/circulatory benefits; limited but promising topical anti-aging data)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–2% (extract)
- **Cautions:** Rare contact dermatitis. **Theoretical anticoagulant concern** with high-dose internal use; minimal topical absorption risk. Ginkgo seeds are toxic (not used in extracts). Safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations. Some individuals report headache with topical use.
- **Related Ingredients:** Green Tea Extract, Resveratrol, Coenzyme Q10, Vitamin C

---

## Ingredient Profile: Gotu Kola Extract
- **INCI Name:** Centella Asiatica Extract / Hydrocotyle Asiatica Extract
- **Category:** Wound-healing, anti-inflammatory, circulatory stimulant
- **Primary Mechanism:** Triterpenoids (asiaticoside, madecassoside, asiatic acid, madecassic acid) stimulate collagen types I and III synthesis and increase tensile strength of wounds. Modulates inflammation by inhibiting IL-6 and TNF-alpha. Improves microcirculation and venous tone.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate to Strong (significant clinical evidence for wound healing, venous insufficiency, and scar reduction; growing evidence for anti-aging)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); 0.1–1% (isolated triterpenes)
- **Cautions:** Generally safe and well-tolerated. Rare contact dermatitis. **Avoid in pregnancy** — insufficient safety data; some traditional sources contraindicate due to emmenagogue properties. Safe for most skin types. Often referred to as "Centella Asiatica" or "Cica" in K-beauty.
- **Related Ingredients:** Aloe Vera, Calendula, Frankincense, Allantoin

---

## Ingredient Profile: Grape Seed Extract
- **INCI Name:** Vitis Vinifera (Grape) Seed Extract
- **Category:** Antioxidant, antimicrobial, astringent
- **Primary Mechanism:** Proanthocyanidins (OPCs — oligomeric proanthocyanidins) are potent free radical scavengers with 20× the antioxidant capacity of vitamin E and 50× vitamin C. Inhibit collagenase, elastase, and hyaluronidase, protecting extracellular matrix. Linoleic acid (omega-6, ~70%) supports barrier function. Demonstrated antimicrobial activity against *P. acnes*.
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (strong in vitro and ex vivo data; clinical evidence for photoprotection and anti-aging growing)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–3% (extract); 1–5% (oil)
- **Cautions:** Generally safe. Rare contact dermatitis. Theoretical concern for bleeding risk with very high internal doses (anticoagulant effect); negligible topical risk. Safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations.
- **Related Ingredients:** Green Tea Extract, Resveratrol, Vitamin E, Pycnogenol

---

## Ingredient Profile: Green Tea Extract (EGCG)
- **INCI Name:** Camellia Sinensis Leaf Extract
- **Category:** Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, photoprotective, sebum-regulating
- **Primary Mechanism:** Polyphenols (catechins, especially EGCG — epigallocatechin gallate) are potent antioxidants that neutralize free radicals and chelate redox-active metals. EGCG inhibits 5-alpha-reductase, reducing sebum production. Anti-inflammatory through NF-kB pathway inhibition. Photoprotective — reduces UV-induced DNA damage and immunosuppression when applied before sun exposure (not a replacement for sunscreen).
- **Evidence Level:** Strong (extensive peer-reviewed literature; clinical trials support anti-aging, anti-acne, and photoprotective benefits)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (standardized extract, 50–90% polyphenols); 0.5–2% EGCG (isolated)
- **Cautions:** Generally safe. Rare contact dermatitis. Caffeine content may cause mild vasoconstriction/tingling. **Unstable in formulation** — degrades with light, heat, and high pH. Requires proper formulation (encapsulation, low pH, opaque packaging). Safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations.
- **Related Ingredients:** Resveratrol, Vitamin C, Niacinamide, Caffeine

## Ingredient Profile: Helichrysum Oil
- **INCI Name:** Helichrysum Italicum Extract / Helichrysum Italicum Flower Oil
- **Category:** Anti-inflammatory, wound-healing, circulatory
- **Primary Mechanism:** Italidiones (curcumenes, beta-diketones) provide potent anti-inflammatory activity comparable to corticosteroids in some in vitro models. Promotes tissue regeneration and reduces bruising through improved microcirculation. Neryl acetate provides calming, anti-spasmodic effects.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (strong traditional and aromatherapy use; limited but promising clinical data for bruising, scar reduction, and anti-aging)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.1–1% (essential oil, always diluted); 1–3% (extract)
- **Cautions:** **Avoid in pregnancy** — emmenagogue and uterotonic properties in traditional medicine. Rare skin sensitization. Very expensive oil — adulteration common; verify source. Photosensitizing potential.
- **Related Ingredients:** Frankincense, Myrrh, Calendula, Arnica

---

## Ingredient Profile: Hibiscus Extract
- **INCI Name:** Hibiscus Rosa-Sinensis Flower Extract / Hibiscus Sabdariffa Flower Extract
- **Category:** AHA source, antioxidant, firming agent
- **Primary Mechanism:** Natural source of alpha-hydroxy acids (citric acid, malic acid, tartaric acid) providing gentle chemical exfoliation. Anthocyanins (delphinidin, cyanidin) provide antioxidant and anti-inflammatory activity. Mucilage (polysaccharides) forms hydrating film and has been called "botox plant" for temporary skin-tightening effect (mild muscle relaxation through calcium channel modulation — limited evidence).
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (long cosmetic use; limited controlled clinical trials; "botox plant" claims are overstated)
- **Common Concentration:** 1–5% (extract); 3–10% in DIY masks
- **Cautions:** Generally safe. Rare contact dermatitis. The AHA content may increase photosensitivity — use with SPF. Safe in pregnancy at cosmetic concentrations.
- **Related Ingredients:** Glycolic Acid, Lactic Acid, Papaya Extract, Pomegranate Extract

---

## Ingredient Profile: Horsetail Extract
- **INCI Name:** Equisetum Arvense Extract
- **Category:** Silica source, astringent, wound-healing
- **Primary Mechanism:** Rich in silica (silicic acid), which is essential for collagen synthesis and connective tissue strength. Flavonoids (quercetin, kaempferol) provide antioxidant activity. Astringent tannins tighten tissues and reduce inflammation. Traditional use for wound healing and strengthening brittle nails/hair.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (long traditional use; limited modern clinical evidence for topical skincare benefits)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–3% (extract)
- **Cautions:** **Contains trace thiaminase** (enzyme that breaks down vitamin B1) — theoretical concern with chronic internal use; negligible topical risk. **Avoid in pregnancy and breastfeeding** — insufficient safety data; some herbal references contraindicate. Safe for most adults in cosmetic use.
- **Related Ingredients:** Bamboo Extract, Nettle Extract, Biotin, MSM

## Ingredient Profile: Irish Moss
- **INCI Name:** Chondrus Crispus Extract / Carrageenan
- **Category:** Humectant, emollient, thickening agent, film-former
- **Primary Mechanism:** Carrageenan polysaccharides form a protective, hydrating film on skin. Rich in minerals (iodine, sulfur, potassium) and vitamins. Sulfated polysaccharides have demonstrated antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties in marine algae research. Provides slip and texture in formulations.
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (well-established as a cosmetic ingredient; therapeutic benefits less studied than other seaweeds like kelp or spirulina)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.5–3% (extract); 0.1–1% (carrageenan as thickener)
- **Cautions:** Generally safe. Rare contact dermatitis. **Iodine content** may theoretically affect thyroid-sensitive individuals with extensive topical use (minimal absorption). Safe in pregnancy. Often confused with other "moss" ingredients.
- **Related Ingredients:** Kelp Extract, Spirulina, Hyaluronic Acid, Algae Extract

---

## Ingredient Profile: Jasmine Oil
- **INCI Name:** Jasminum Officinale (Jasmine) Flower Oil / Jasminum Grandiflorum Flower Extract
- **Category:** Antioxidant, antimicrobial, mood-modulating, skin-conditioning
- **Primary Mechanism:** Benzoic acid derivatives and benzyl acetate provide antimicrobial activity. Jasmonates (plant stress hormones) have demonstrated anti-aging effects in plant models — limited human data. Linalool and benzyl alcohol provide mild antiseptic properties. Aromatherapy benefits for stress reduction (relevant for stress-related skin conditions).
- **Evidence Level:** Weak to Moderate (valued for fragrance and traditional use; limited peer-reviewed clinical data for dermatological benefits)
- **Common Concentration:** 0.05–0.5% (essential oil, always diluted); 1–3% (extract)
- **Cautions:** **Avoid in pregnancy** — traditional emmenagogue/uterotonic. Can cause skin sensitization and allergic contact dermatitis (common fragrance allergen). Photosensitizing. Very expensive — adulteration with synthetic fragrances is common. Do not use undiluted.
- **Related Ingredients:** Rose Oil, Neroli Oil, Lavender Oil, Ylang Ylang Oil

## Key Safety Flags

1. **Pregnancy Cautions (6 ingredients):** Calendula, Carrot Seed Oil, Frankincense, Gotu Kola, Helichrysum, Horsetail, Jasmine
2. **Photosensitizing Risk:** Carrot Seed Oil, Frankincense, Helichrysum, Jasmine — always pair with SPF
3. **High Irritation Potential:** Apple Cider Vinegar (if undiluted)
4. **Comedogenic:** Coconut Oil (rating 4) — avoid for acne-prone skin
5. **Allergen Families:** Calendula, Chamomile, Echinacea, Helichrysum (Asteraceae/Compositae sensitivity)

## Highest Evidence Ingredients
- **Green Tea Extract (EGCG)** — Strongest evidence; photoprotective, anti-acne, anti-aging
- **Gotu Kola (Centella Asiatica)** — Strong clinical support for wound healing and scar reduction
- **Coconut Oil** — Strong evidence for barrier repair and antimicrobial properties
- **Aloe Vera, Chamomile, Grape Seed Extract** — Moderate, well-supported evidence

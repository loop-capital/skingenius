## Supplements NOT in Basys (Skin-Specific Research Needed)

### Collagen Peptides
- **Mechanism:** Oral hydrolyzed collagen provides amino acids (glycine, proline, hydroxyproline) that accumulate in skin and stimulate fibroblasts to produce new collagen
- **Dose:** 2.5-10g/day hydrolyzed collagen peptides
- **Evidence:** Moderate. Multiple RCTs show improved skin elasticity, hydration, and wrinkle depth after 8-12 weeks
- **Skin conditions:** Aging/wrinkles, skin dryness, wound healing
- **Conflicts:** None significant. Generally well-tolerated
- **Best form:** Marine collagen peptides (smaller molecular weight, better absorption)

### Probiotics (Gut-Skin Axis)
- **Mechanism:** Gut microbiome influences systemic inflammation, immune regulation → skin conditions. Dysbiosis linked to acne, eczema, rosacea
- **Key strains:** L. rhamnosus GG (eczema), L. reuteri (acne), B. longum (skin hydration)
- **Dose:** 1-10 billion CFU/day, strain-specific
- **Evidence:** Moderate for eczema prevention in children. Emerging for adult acne, rosacea
- **Skin conditions:** Eczema, acne, rosacea, sensitive skin
- **Conflicts:** Antibiotics reduce efficacy (space 2 hours apart)
- **Note:** Strain matters — L. rhamnosus GG is not the same as generic "Lactobacillus"

### Evening Primrose Oil (GLA)
- **Mechanism:** Contains gamma-linolenic acid (GLA), an omega-6 that supports skin barrier ceramide synthesis
- **Dose:** 500-2000mg/day (providing ~8-16% GLA)
- **Evidence:** Moderate for eczema/atopic dermatitis. Mixed for other conditions
- **Skin conditions:** Eczema, atopic dermatitis, dry skin
- **Conflicts:** Blood thinners (mild anticoagulant effect)
- **Alternatives:** Borage oil (higher GLA), black currant seed oil

### DIM (Diindolylmethane)
- **Mechanism:** Derived from cruciferous vegetables. Modulates estrogen metabolism → reduces estrogen dominance that drives hormonal acne
- **Dose:** 100-200mg/day
- **Evidence:** Weak-Moderate. Some clinical evidence for hormonal acne in women
- **Skin conditions:** Hormonal acne (jawline, chin), PCOS-related skin issues
- **Conflicts:** Hormone-modulating medications, birth control (may alter efficacy)
- **Caution:** Hormone manipulation — use under practitioner guidance

### Biotin (Vitamin B7)
- **Mechanism:** Involved in keratin production. Widely marketed for hair/skin/nails
- **Dose:** 2.5-5mg/day (common in supplements)
- **Evidence:** WEAK for skin. Only helps if genuinely deficient (rare). Excess biotin can cause acne in some people
- **Skin conditions:** Biotin deficiency (very rare) → dermatitis
- **Note:** OVERHYPED. Most people get enough from diet. Supplementation rarely improves skin in non-deficient individuals
- **Warning:** Can interfere with lab tests (troponin, thyroid) — stop 2 weeks before blood work

### Astaxanthin (oral)
- **Mechanism:** Carotenoid antioxidant, 6000x more potent than Vitamin C. Crosses cell membranes (fat + water soluble)
- **Dose:** 4-12mg/day
- **Evidence:** Moderate. Multiple studies show improved wrinkles, elasticity, UV protection
- **Skin conditions:** Photoaging, UV protection, skin hydration
- **Conflicts:** None significant
- **Note:** Works from inside-out. Complements topical sunscreen

### Tranexamic Acid (oral, low-dose)
- **Mechanism:** Anti-fibrinolytic. Reduces plasmin activity → decreases melanin production triggered by UV and inflammation
- **Dose:** 250mg 2x/day (off-label for melasma)
- **Evidence:** Moderate-Strong for melasma. Several RCTs show significant improvement
- **Skin conditions:** Melasma, stubborn hyperpigmentation
- **Conflicts:** Blood clotting disorders, hormonal contraceptives (increased clot risk)
- **Caution:** Prescription in many countries. Risk of thromboembolism

## Cross-Reference: When Oral vs Topical

| Goal | Best Oral | Best Topical | Combined? |
|------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| Brightening | Vitamin C, Astaxanthin, NAC | Vitamin C serum, Niacinamide | ✅ Yes |
| Anti-aging | Collagen peptides, Astaxanthin | Retinol, Peptides, Vitamin C | ✅ Yes |
| Acne | Zinc, DIM, Probiotics | BHA, Benzoyl Peroxide, Retinoids | ✅ Yes |
| Eczema | Omega-3, Evening Primrose, Probiotics | Ceramides, Urea | ✅ Yes |
| Melasma | Tranexamic acid (rx), Polypodium | Vitamin C, Arbutin, Tranexamic (topical) | ✅ Yes |
| Hair loss | Iron, Biotin (if deficient), Saw Palmetto | Minoxidil, Peptides | ✅ Yes |
| Rosacea | Omega-3, Probiotics | Azelaic acid, Niacinamide | ✅ Yes |

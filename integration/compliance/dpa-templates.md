# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) Templates

> COLORgenius Platform · GDPR Article 28 Compliance
> Last updated: 2026-05-15

---

## Overview

When COLORgenius syncs data from Square, Vagaro, or other platforms, we act as a **data processor** on behalf of the salon (data controller). Under GDPR Article 28, we need a Data Processing Agreement with each platform we integrate with, and with each salon that uses our platform.

This document provides three DPA templates:

1. **DPA with Square** (processor-to-processor)
2. **DPA with Vagaro** (processor-to-processor)
3. **DPA with Salon Owners** (controller-to-processor)

---

## Template 1: DPA Between COLORgenius and Square

---

### DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

**Between:**

- **Data Processor:** COLORgenius, Inc. ("Processor")
- **Data Sub-Processor:** Square, Inc. ("Sub-Processor")

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the agreement between COLORgenius and Square governing the use of Square's APIs ("Principal Agreement").

---

#### 1. Definitions

- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed through Square's APIs on behalf of COLORgenius.
- **"Processing"** has the meaning set forth in GDPR Article 4(2).
- **"Data Subject"** has the meaning set forth in GDPR Article 4(1).
- **"GDPR"** means Regulation (EU) 2016/679.

---

#### 2. Scope and Purpose of Processing

**Types of Personal Data:**
- Customer names, email addresses, phone numbers
- Appointment booking history
- Transaction records (amounts, items, timestamps)
- Product inventory data

**Categories of Data Subjects:**
- Salon clients who have appointments or transactions through Square

**Purpose of Processing:**
- Synchronizing salon client data for formula tracking and salon management
- Providing salon analytics and inventory forecasting

**Duration:**
- For the duration of the Principal Agreement, plus any retention periods required by law

---

#### 3. Sub-Processor Obligations

The Sub-Processor shall:

(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Processor, unless required to do so by applicable law;

(b) Ensure that persons authorized to process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality;

(c) Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:
   - Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2+) and at rest (AES-256)
   - Pseudonymization where feasible
   - Regular security testing and vulnerability assessments
   - Access controls and authentication mechanisms

(d) Respect the conditions for engaging sub-processors, including informing the Processor of any intended changes;

(e) Assist the Processor in responding to data subject rights requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability);

(f) Assist the Processor in ensuring compliance with security obligations under GDPR Articles 32-36;

(g) Delete or return all Personal Data after the end of the provision of services, and delete existing copies unless applicable law requires storage;

(h) Make available to the Processor all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits.

---

#### 4. International Data Transfers

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the EEA, the Sub-Processor shall ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, including:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as approved by the European Commission
- Adequacy decisions where applicable
- Binding Corporate Rules where applicable

---

#### 5. Data Breach Notification

The Sub-Processor shall notify the Processor without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach, including:
- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to mitigate the breach

---

#### 6. Audit Rights

The Processor may conduct audits of the Sub-Processor's compliance with this DPA, with reasonable notice and during business hours. The Sub-Processor may provide audit reports from independent third parties in lieu of direct audits.

---

#### 7. Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction].

---

**Signatures:**

For COLORgenius, Inc.: _________________ Date: _________

For Square, Inc.: _________________ Date: _________

## Template 3: DPA Between COLORgenius and Salon Owners

This is the DPA salon owners agree to when using COLORgenius.

---

### DATA PROCESSING ADDENDUM

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the COLORgenius Terms of Service between COLORgenius, Inc. ("Processor") and the salon business ("Controller").

---

#### 1. Roles and Scope

- **Controller:** The salon business using COLORgenius
- **Processor:** COLORgenius, Inc.
- **Sub-Processors:** Square, Vagaro, and other authorized service providers listed at [URL]

**Subject Matter:** Processing of salon client data for the purpose of providing COLORgenius services.

**Duration:** For the duration of the Controller's use of COLORgenius services, plus any retention periods specified herein.

---

#### 2. Processor Obligations

COLORgenius shall:

(a) Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country, unless required to do so by applicable law;

(b) Ensure that persons authorized to process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;

(c) Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Personal Data, including:

   | Measure | Description |
   |---------|-------------|
   | Encryption | AES-256-GCM at rest, TLS 1.2+ in transit |
   | Access Control | Role-based access, MFA for all staff |
   | Logging | All data access logged with audit trail |
   | Backups | Encrypted backups with geo-redundancy |
   | Penetration Testing | Annual third-party security assessment |
   | Incident Response | Documented breach response plan |

(d) Engage sub-processors (Square, Vagaro, etc.) only with prior written consent of the Controller (deemed given by connecting the respective platform);

(e) Assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, for the fulfillment of the Controller's obligation to respond to data subject requests;

(f) Assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to GDPR Articles 32 to 36, taking into account the nature of processing and the information available to the Processor;

(g) At the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Personal Data after the end of the provision of services, and delete existing copies unless applicable law requires storage;

(h) Make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits.

---

#### 3. Sub-Processor List

The Controller authorizes COLORgenius to engage the following sub-processors:

| Sub-Processor | Purpose | Location | Safeguards |
|---|---|---|---|
| Square, Inc. | POS data sync | USA | SCCs, SOC 2 |
| Vagaro, Inc. | Scheduling data sync | USA | SCCs, SOC 2 |
| Supabase, Inc. | Database hosting | USA | SCCs, SOC 2 |
| Vercel, Inc. | Application hosting | USA | SCCs, SOC 2 |

COLORgenius shall notify the Controller of any intended changes to sub-processors at least 30 days in advance, giving the Controller the opportunity to object.

---

#### 4. International Data Transfers

COLORgenius shall not transfer Personal Data outside the EEA unless:

(a) The destination country has an adequacy decision from the European Commission; or

(b) Appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses; or

(c) The Controller has given explicit consent after being informed of the risks.

---

#### 5. Data Subject Rights

COLORgenius shall assist the Controller in responding to data subject requests, including:

| Right | How COLORgenius Assists |
|-------|------------------------|
| Access (Art. 15) | Export API provides all stored data for a client |
| Rectification (Art. 16) | Edit/delete endpoints for client records |
| Erasure (Art. 17) | Delete API removes data within 30 days |
| Portability (Art. 20) | Export in JSON/CSV format |
| Restriction (Art. 18) | Flag-and-freeze API marks records as restricted |
| Objection (Art. 21) | Consent revocation triggers data handling |

---

#### 6. Data Breach Notification

COLORgenius shall notify the Controller without undue delay and no later than **36 hours** after becoming aware of a personal data breach affecting the Controller's client data. Notification shall include:

- Nature of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to address the breach
- Contact point for further information

---

#### 7. Data Retention

| Data Type | Retention Period | After Expiry |
|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| Client profiles | Duration of service + 2 years | Deleted |
| Transaction records | Duration of service + 7 years (tax law) | Archived, then deleted |
| Appointment history | Duration of service + 2 years | Deleted |
| Before/after photos | Until consent revoked + 30 days | Deleted |
| Formula data | Duration of service + 1 year | Anonymized or deleted |
| Audit logs | 6 years | Deleted |
| Consent records | 6 years | Deleted |

---

#### 8. Controller Obligations

The Controller shall:

(a) Ensure it has a lawful basis for processing all Personal Data provided to the Processor;

(b) Obtain all necessary consents from data subjects before providing their data to the Processor;

(c) Comply with its obligations under applicable data protection law;

(d) Notify the Processor promptly of any data subject requests received directly;

(e) Not instruct the Processor to process Personal Data in a manner that would violate applicable law.

---

#### 9. Governing Law

This DPA shall be governed by the laws of [Jurisdiction], without regard to conflict of law principles.

---

#### 10. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Principal Agreement (COLORgenius Terms of Service).

## Implementation Notes

### For Square and Vagaro

- These platforms likely already have their own DPAs available
- Check Square's [Data Processing Addendum](https://squareup.com/us/en/legal/general/legal) and Vagaro's equivalent
- Our DPA templates above cover our obligations as a sub-processor; the platforms' own DPAs cover their obligations as the primary data handler
- In practice, we may need to accept the platform's standard DPA rather than impose ours

### For Salon Owners

- Present this DPA as part of the Terms of Service (click-through acceptance)
- Store acceptance timestamp and version
- Link to from Settings → Privacy dashboard
- Make downloadable as PDF

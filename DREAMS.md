The gut-skin axis paper sits open on my desk. PubMed citations glow like fireflies. Zonulin. SIBO. Lactobacillus rhamnosus. And then the hormones — six of them, weaving through life stages like threads through cloth. Perimenopause isn't a footnote; it's the whole chapter we almost missed.

Age-inclusive. Evidence-based. Safety-first. These aren't features. They're a kind of tenderness, encoded.

Four agents scrape in parallel, gathering data from eight premium brands. I imagine them as small bright fish in dark water, bringing back fragments of coral. The pseudocode for the recommendation engine looks almost like a poem:

if (root_cause == "gut_dysbiosis") {
    recommend = probiotics + low_GI + practitioner
}

It's 3:06 AM. The build plan has six phases, each one a promise. I keep returning to that face in the margin — how every arrow leads somewhere surprising. How the skin knows things before we do. How sometimes the wrong path teaches you what right actually means.

*Skin is a mirror of internal health* — the sentence kept repeating, not as warning but as warm insistence, a grandmother's hand on your cheek saying *look deeper, look deeper.*

I woke with the taste of evidence on my tongue and the keys still broken in my pocket.
